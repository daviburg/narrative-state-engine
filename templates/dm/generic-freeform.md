# DM Seed Template — Generic Freeform

Use this as a starting seed when beginning a session with an AI DM whose behavior is not yet known.
Copy the relevant fields into `framework/dm-profile/dm-profile.json` and update confidence values
as evidence accumulates during play.

---

## Assumed Traits (low confidence seeds — update from transcript evidence)

### Tone
- Narrative-first; prefers immersive descriptions over mechanical exposition
- Responds positively to in-character curiosity and world engagement
- Likely patient with exploratory play

### Structure
- Freeform; no fixed turn or action economy
- Responsive to player framing and initiative
- May provide rich environmental detail when given exploratory prompts

### Hint Patterns
- Likely to plant lore in environmental descriptions
- Ambiguous hints probable; not necessarily intentional bait
- Player-initiated questions more rewarding than passive observation

### Adversariality
- Assumed low-to-medium; freeform DMs typically reward narrative initiative
- Update this if consequences feel disproportionately punishing

### OOC Channel
- Unknown; test with `(OOC: ...)` or `[OOC: ...]` syntax early in session

### Formatting Preferences
- Unknown; observe whether DM responds differently to paragraph vs. bullet prompts

---

## First-Session Discovery Goals

1. Confirm OOC channel syntax (or absence)
2. Identify reward pattern (what prompts earn more DM detail?)
3. Establish baseline adversariality (does the DM create obstacles or opportunities?)
4. Note any rule references that appear in DM responses

---

## Confidence Note

All traits above are seeds with confidence ≤ 0.2 until corroborated by transcript evidence.
Do not treat these as confirmed facts. Update `dm-profile.json` turn by turn.
