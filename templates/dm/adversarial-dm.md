# DM Seed Template — Adversarial DM

Use when early transcript evidence suggests the DM is creating obstacles, exploiting player mistakes,
or actively steering the narrative against player objectives. This seed raises the baseline risk estimate.

---

## Assumed Traits (low confidence seeds — confirm from evidence before relying on)

### Tone
- Challenge-first; player victories must be earned through careful play
- Consequences for recklessness
- May punish meta-game reasoning if exposed

### Structure
- Likely tracks player commitments closely
- Irreversible actions may be enforced strictly
- Ambiguity often resolved against the player

### Hint Patterns
- High dm_bait probability; convenient opportunities may be traps
- Volunteered information should be treated as inference until cross-verified
- Pattern-test hints with low-commitment probes before acting

### Adversariality
- Assumed high; upgrade all risk classifications by one level
- Verify every assumption before committing resources or relationships

### OOC Channel
- May not exist or may be discouraged
- Test cautiously; an adversarial DM may penalise meta-queries in-narrative

### Formatting Preferences
- Unknown; adversarial DMs may exploit ambiguous prompt framing
- Be precise; avoid open-ended prompts that can be resolved against player intent

---

## First-Session Discovery Goals

1. Establish actual adversariality level — seed may be miscalibrated
2. Identify bait patterns specific to this DM
3. Map which actions have been punished and which rewarded
4. Find any OOC escape valve for high-stakes decisions

---

## Risk Adjustment

With an adversarial DM seed:
- Treat all medium-risk actions as high-risk until DM is better understood
- Require two corroborating signals before acting on any single hint
- Prefer probing and reversible actions until a clearer picture emerges

---

## Confidence Note

Adversarial DM classification itself may be premature. Maintain this seed only if evidence supports it.
If evidence accumulates for balanced or permissive behavior, switch to the appropriate seed template.
