# turn-231 — DM

The man, his eyes still wide with a glimmer of hope, begins to speak, but you cut him off gently. "Slowly," you instruct, your voice a calm anchor in the damp air. "You are not dying in this moment." You watch his shallow, ragged breathing, noting the slight tremor in his hands. Hope, you know, makes people speak quickly. Truth often takes its time. You gesture to the satchel, his sole possession. "Open it. Slowly. Place what you carry on the ground." His movements are clumsy, fumbling, his fingers stiff with cold and weakness. The leather bag falls once, then twice, before he manages to untie the thong and spill its meager contents onto the thawing earth. A single, dull eating knife, a small pouch of dried, unrecognizable roots, and a crudely carved wooden figurine, its features worn smooth, are all that emerge. No symbols, no script, no hidden meanings leap out from these simple, personal items. "Water," you command the healer, turning only slightly. "Do not touch him. Leave it within reach." The healer, well-trained, places a filled waterskin carefully on the ground, just beyond the man's immediate grasp. With immense effort, he lunges forward, seizing it with both hands, bringing it to his lips. He gulps greedily, some of the precious liquid spilling down his chin, but the act visibly drains him, leaving him gasping for breath. "You walked alone," you state, your eyes piercing. "The land told me that. No one followed you. No one hunted you." Another deliberate pause hangs in the air, heavy with unspoken accusation. "So I ask again -- carefully this time -- what did you leave behind that did this to you?" He coughs then, a wet, rattling sound that seems to tear at his chest. The red splotches on his face, though not visibly spreading, seem to deepen in hue with the effort. His voice, when it finally comes, is a hoarse whisper, weakened by the exertion. "The fever... it took them all... my family... the village... I fled... to outrun it..." His eyes, filled with a primal terror, dart around, as if expecting the unseen menace to materialize from the melting snow. His condition worsens, if only slightly, his frame shaking with a fresh wave of chills. You kneel, not closer to him, but closer to the earth between you both, placing your hand on the cold, damp soil. You reach outward, your senses seeking the smallest creatures nearby: the earthworms stirring beneath the thawing soil, the insects awakening from their winter slumber. You ask: "Have others fallen like this? Do bodies remain where he came from? Does the sickness move--or stay?" The earth responds in hushed, subterranean tremors, a chorus of tiny, frantic vibrations. Yes, they whisper, many have fallen. The earth drinks them, countless still. The sickness, they confirm, does not move of its own accord. It settles, consumes, and leaves stillness in its wake. It is the living who carry it, fleeing its grip, spreading its silent, invisible touch.
