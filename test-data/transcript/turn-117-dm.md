# turn-117 — DM

You seek out the shaman, finding him tending to a small, flickering flame within his ornate dwelling, a place of quiet contemplation. You approach him with deference, and though no common tongue binds you, your eyes, and a series of gentle gestures towards the worn implements and draughty shelters, convey your intent to aid. He watches, his flinty gaze assessing, before offering a slow, thoughtful nod of understanding. You begin with the women, focusing your subtle energies on their daily tasks. A simple mending needle, its eye too small for their coarse thread, glows faintly as you enhance its sharpness and strength, allowing it to pierce hides with greater ease. The brittle hides themselves, used for tent repairs, become marginally more pliant, less prone to tearing as your subtle magic works its way into their fibers. You demonstrate, through example, the small, practical ways your power can ease their burdens, working quietly alongside them, waiting patiently for any sign of acknowledgment or further invitation, particularly from the shaman, whose presence you feel observing your efforts.
