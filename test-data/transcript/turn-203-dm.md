# turn-203 — DM

You kneel beside the hexagonal melt pattern, its precise geometry stark against the ragged snow. With the butt of your staff, you trace its edge, feeling a subtle, invisible resistance. The pattern, though seemingly ephemeral, acts as if carved from solid material; it resists correction, the snow around it refusing to yield or merge into a natural curve. When disturbed, it doesn't collapse but slowly, imperceptibly, begins to reassert its perfect angles, as if imprinted into the very fabric of the land. Rurik, ever efficient, uses a leather strip to carefully collect one of the dark fragments, depositing it into a bone charm pouch, its cold presence contained but distinct. You study it with arcane sight: it pulses with a faint, low thrum, a mirror to the mountain's distant beat, yet its energy seems self-contained, only subtly diminishing within the pouch. It reacts to your pact energy not with resonance, but a profound, almost aggressive neutrality, as if your life-force were utterly irrelevant to its purpose. Neither cold iron nor obsidian evokes any specific reaction, further cementing its alien nature. Turning to the crystalline growths, you break one carefully with the flat of your blade. It fractures with a sharp, brittle *ping*, like thin glass, without light or further sound. The pieces fall, sharp and crystalline, yet the underlying energy boundary remains undisturbed, suggesting these are mere excretions, not integral components. Before the first light of dawn, you carve subtle markings into the snow, charting the present edge of the sterile boundary, a map to compare against tomorrow's observation. Rurik, under your direction, buries the remaining fragments beneath a marked stone, while you secure a thumb-joint sized piece for closer study. Your objective is not conquest, but understanding. You test the edges of this new, unsettling reality.
