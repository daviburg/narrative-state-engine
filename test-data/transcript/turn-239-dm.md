# turn-239 — DM

The pattern, so perfectly consistent, settled like a shroud over the ailing man. "That is not chance," you murmur, more to yourself than to Kael or the healer. "It is either contained... or sustained." You do not change the treatment; instead, you begin to test the very nature of the affliction. First, you test distance. The fragment, cold and humming, remains at the longhouse. Without direct contact, the man is carefully moved slightly farther from the settlement, increasing the gap between him and the subtle arcane influence. You observe for a full interval, marking his breath, the fever's heat, the spread of the marks. Nothing changes. His condition holds its grim, steady course, unaffected by the increased separation. The sickness, it seems, does not draw its strength from the mountain's ambient power. Next, you test controlled resonance. You call Anya, who stands at a safe distance, her breath misting in the cool air. "Repeat the line," you instruct, "same motion. Same control." She traces the familiar frost symbol, a delicate, intricate pattern shimmering in the air. You watch the man's chest. His breathing does not alter, the fever remains a constant internal fire, and the marks on his skin hold their angry, unyielding spread. Her sharpened power, even amplified by the fragment, has no discernible effect. The sickness is not purely natural, but it does not react to this particular arcane touch. Finally, you test the environment. A controlled, slight increase in warmth is introduced to his shelter. For a brief span, his ragged breathing seems to ease, a flicker of something almost akin to comfort crosses his face, but the red marks on his skin seem to intensify, almost glowing. When the warmth is returned to neutral, his agitated state slowly recedes, the pattern of his suffering resuming its steady rhythm. The fever, it ignores neither change, reacting to the physical shifts in his surroundings. You step back, your gaze lingering on the man, then briefly toward the distant fragment. "If it is natural," you say quietly to Kael, "it will follow the body. If it is arcane..." You glance toward the fragment. "...it will answer something else." You do not guess. You make it reveal itself.
