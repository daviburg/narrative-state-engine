# turn-201 — DM

The thrumming pulse in the mountains fades, but its echo remains in your senses. You lower your hand from the obsidian, turning your gaze to the stark, frozen landscape stretching between your camp and the distant, glowing peaks. Rurik and Boruk remain vigilant behind you, their expressions serious as you move, scanning the ground with renewed intensity. Your arcane sight, sharpened by the recent vision and the raw pulse of energy, immediately picks out anomalies. Not far from the camp, a patch of snow, larger than any natural drift, lies with an unnaturally perfect hexagonal melt pattern at its center, as if a geometric force had been pressed upon it. Further on, a series of small, crystalline growths glitter in the faint moonlight, not ice but something harder, purer, almost metallic in their sheen. You find small, dark fragments embedded in the snow, irregularly shaped and cool to the touch, yet radiating that same sterile, alien energy you first sensed. The sparse arctic vegetation, particularly the hardy mosses and lichens, seems to recoil from an invisible boundary line, leaving a barren strip that snakes towards the mountains. Even the air itself, in certain pockets, feels thinner, colder, as if the life had been subtly leached from it. You don't climb the mountain tonight; you trace its chilling influence, mapping the subtle arteries of its power across the frozen earth, each anomaly a piece of a vast, incomprehensible puzzle.
