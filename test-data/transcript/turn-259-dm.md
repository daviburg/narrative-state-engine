# turn-259 — DM

"Before first light becomes departure," you declare, standing before the assembled tribe, the southern shamans, and your chosen companions, "we name what is real." First, the teaching. The southern shamans, under Anya's unyielding gaze and the healer's precise guidance, conduct their first controlled application of the method. On a patient they brought--a young man from their own tribe, exhibiting early, unmistakable signs of the geometric plague--they perform with meticulous care. The boundary holds, the timing is followed with unwavering discipline, and their precision, though less fluid than Anya's, is undeniable. As the faint arc of frost cuts the pattern's leading edge, the patient's fevered tremor eases, and the green luminescence within his lesions momentarily dims. A collective, hopeful gasp ripples through the onlookers. They have not only understood but have begun to wield the method. Trust, fragile but real, begins to bloom. Second, the witness. You sit with the survivor, his strength slowly returning, his hands no longer trembling as he traces the patterns of his past onto a piece of smoothed bark. His map is stark, brutal in its clarity. It marks his village, a cluster of scorched earth and empty longhouses to the west, then the terrifying, zigzagging path of the sickness's spread, following faint trade routes. His flight eastward, marked by points where he encountered the strange "glowing stones" near a particular river bend, is laid out with desperate precision, leading inevitably towards the distant mountain. He has given you a true path, a terrifying blueprint of devastation. Third, the fragment. With the Shaman, you observe it one final time. Its hum is deeper now, no longer merely cold but resonant, almost vibrant. It seems to react to the shared knowledge in the air, the collective intent, and the faint, residual energy of the cured man. It doesn't overtly pull eastward, but its rhythm has synchronized, a subtle yet undeniable echo of the distant mountain's ancient pulse. "It responds to the awakening," the Shaman murmurs, his eyes wide with a new understanding. "As the land stirs, so does it." Fourth, the equipment. Laid out on a clean hide, each item is examined. The reinforced head covering, a multi-layered hood of treated furs and bone, feels snug and offers excellent protection without impeding sight or sound. Borin's short-hafted hammer-axe is a balanced piece of utility, capable of blocking a blow, prying, or even splitting wood, silent and sturdy. The simple sling, paired with a pouch of carefully selected river stones, is a quiet, reliable ranged option. From the southern shamans, you select a few woven talismans and carved bone fetishes that, under your arcane sight, hum with a grounding, pattern-disrupting energy. The healer's compact pouch contains all essential, tested supplies: concentrated broths, purified snowmelt, and potent immunity-boosting essences. Nothing is weak, awkward, loud, or untrusted. You depart with what works. Fifth, the expedition. Rurik, stoic and efficient, checks his hunting gear one last time. The tracker, a lean figure whose eyes seem to see more in the shifting patterns of snow than most, adjusts his bindings. The scout, a whisper of a presence, melts into the shadows at the edge of the camp, already anticipating the path ahead. Each carries only what is essential, each understands the boundary, the sickness, and the solemn rule: to learn, not to prove courage. They are ready. All five are answered. "We move at first light," you declare, your voice resonating with resolve. The world waits, an unknown challenge ahead, but you and your companions are prepared to meet it.
