# turn-225 — DM

The first true signs of thaw touched the land with tentative fingers, carrying with them a new scent--the earthy promise of damp soil beneath the snow. Yet, this promise was accompanied by an approaching shadow. On the horizon, a lone figure emerged, struggling through the breaking snow, heading directly for the longhouse. Not a hunter, not a foe, but a traveler, whose every faltering step spoke of profound weakness and encroaching sickness. You gather the council as soon as the figure is clearly visible. "Winter has released its grip," you announce, your gaze sweeping over Kael, the Shaman, Lena, and the senior hunters. "And with it, a new presence approaches." You describe the figure, the tell-tale signs of illness evident even from a distance. "We prepare to move again, to follow the rivers once they begin to break, to seek the trapper's route and the rumor of glowing stones. We will reassess the southern tribes as movement returns, and decide whether to approach the mountain again with what we have learned." You observe the fragment on the stone, its hum quickening subtly with the thaw, and Anya, whose frost tracing is almost effortlessly precise today. The mountain's influence, it seems, shifted with the world's cycles. But the immediate concern is the ailing stranger. "This," you say, gesturing towards the approaching figure, "is our first encounter with the wider world. We must meet it with wisdom and caution." The spring, it seems, begins with a test of compassion and vigilance.
