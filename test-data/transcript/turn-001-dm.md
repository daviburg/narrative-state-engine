# turn-001 — DM

You come to with your face in the snow. When you push yourself up, the world tilts - dark trees, the setting sun, it all starts to slide until you steady yourself. Breath fogging the air in shallow bursts, you take in the faint ribbon of a path and the slick sheen of ice you clearly didn't see in time. Your temple throbs where you must have hit the ground. Bits of memory slot back into place as you gather yourself. Lanterns and pine wreaths. The worried faces in the village hall. A promised reward. The... what was it called again? You think you'd forgotten the name even before hitting you head, but regardless, it was taken - and it was important enough that without it, the Midwinter celebration can't go on. Traditions missed. Songs unsung. A long night made longer by disappointment. Tonight's job is to save a celebration and you'd been following the trail. You still are - ahead, fresh footprints puncture the immaculate, freshly fallen snow, leading off the path and into the trees. What do you do?
