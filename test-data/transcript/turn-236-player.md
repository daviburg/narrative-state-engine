# turn-236 — PLAYER

We do not act further. We observe. Time passes under watch. Not loosely--but measured. --- We mark the passing in clear intervals: first hours, then a half day, then a full day. At each point, we record: - the strength of his breath, - the intensity of the fever, - the spread or stability of the marks, - his ability to drink and remain conscious. We do not change treatment unless something changes first. --- I remain present for the first interval. Then I step back, returning only at each marked time. The healer reports between each. Nothing is assumed. Everything is compared. --- At the same time, the ground is watched. No one approaches the area beneath him unnecessarily. If anything stirs beneath the soil, if the earth shifts, if animals avoid or react-- it is reported immediately. --- We continue this until one of three things happens: - the fever breaks or weakens, - the condition clearly worsens beyond recovery, - or something external changes around him. --- When that moment comes, we stop. And I decide.
