# turn-127 — DM

Your blueprints for the longhouse, etched into the snow, become your focus. You convey your vision of a communal home, grounded and warm, a sanctuary for mothers and infants against the biting cold, to the shaman. His silent approval spurs you on. The first week is a whirlwind of methodical labor. You direct the clearing of the chosen site, using subtle earth-shaping cantrips to loosen frozen soil and clear roots, making the arduous task far less draining for the tribe members who join you. The women, understanding the purpose of your efforts, lend their strength to hauling away debris, their quiet chants accompanying the rhythmic thud of their crude tools. Kael, always close, supervises the careful preservation of towering firs to serve as a natural windbreak. By week's end, the ground is remarkably level, and salvaged stones are meticulously placed along the perimeter, marking the outlines of a central hearth, cozy sleeping alcoves, and dedicated spaces for storage and crafts. As the second week dawns, you initiate the construction of the raised stone perimeter. Large, flat stones are dry-stacked into a foot-high wall, a monumental effort in this land devoid of mortar. The men, guided by Kael, haul the heaviest stones, their powerful shoulders straining. You, in turn, infuse the stones with subtle protective energies, enhancing their cohesion against the frost. The women and elders fill the interior with compacted gravel and sand, creating a solid, insulated floor. Slowly, steadily, the foundation of the longhouse, and a new future, begins to rise from the arctic earth.
