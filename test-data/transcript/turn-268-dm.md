# turn-268 — DM

At first light, the camp was already stirring, but without ceremony. Rurik, a silhouette against the rising sun, took the lead, his hunter's gait eating up the thawing ground. The tracker, a ghost in the periphery, moved like a shadow, while you positioned yourself where the undulating land offered the broadest view, watching the subtle interplay between your companions and the awakening world around you. The trapper's path, though faint beneath the melting snow, was discernible, marked by occasional blazes on ancient pines and small, cairn-like stone piles. As the day wore on, the initial vibrancy of mid-spring began to thin. Life did not vanish entirely, but its presence became more hesitant. Birds, once plentiful, grew scarcer. Small game tracks veered away from certain areas, an instinctual avoidance of something unseen. You began to notice subtle patterns repeating in the landscape itself. Hexagonal melt patterns in the remaining snow, eerily similar to those observed near the mountain, reappeared here and there along the path, even far from its direct influence. Patches of crystalline growths, glinting with a sterile blue light, emerged from the thawing earth in places where the ancient forest gave way to more open tundra. The land, once a canvas of organic chaos, felt subtly ordered, structured by an unseen hand. By late afternoon, the river's roar grew louder, signaling your approach to its banks. The trapper's path descended towards a sharp bend where the water churned violently, carrying chunks of ice and debris. Here, nestled among the tumbling stones of the riverbed, several larger stones pulsed with a faint, internal blue light, exactly as the rumors had described. This was the place. You halt the expedition before nightfall, well before exhaustion could set in. The air, though quiet, feels charged, the silence deeper than natural. The pulsating stones cast an ethereal glow on the churning water, a beacon in the twilight. The world is not empty, you realize, but holding its breath.
