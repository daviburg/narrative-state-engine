# turn-240 — PLAYER

The pattern is too precise. Too consistent. Too... structured. --- I do not move closer. But I change how I look. --- I let the world shift. Not in form--but in meaning. I open my arcane sight fully--not to the fragment, not to the mountain-- but to him. --- I study the man as I would study a tool, or a rune, or a flaw in a crafted thing. Not flesh. Pattern. --- I look for: - whether the fever has a shape, - whether the marks follow a path or a design, - whether his breath carries a rhythm beyond weakness, - whether something within him repeats--not randomly, but with intent. --- I compare silently: This... and the fragment. This... and Anya's frost. This... and the natural life I have learned to read. --- If there is something foreign-- it will not hide from structure. --- I do not touch him. I do not reach into him. I only observe: what in him does not belong to the body that carries it. --- Then, quietly: "Tell me," I say to him, "When the marks appeared... did they come all at once..." "Or did they follow a path?" --- I listen-- not for fear, but for sequence.
