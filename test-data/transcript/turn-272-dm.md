# turn-272 — DM

"What we saw in the body," you muse quietly, your gaze sweeping from the pulsating river stones to the furthest reaches of their subtle influence, "was not the source. It was a reflection. If this place creates the pattern, then the edge is where it will resist least." You choose a point at the outer boundary of the influence, a spot where the hexagonal melt patterns are faint and scattered, the crystalline growths barely a shimmering dust on the thawing earth. Here, the land is still mostly natural, yet undeniably touched. You mark a single, deliberate line in the ground with the tip of your boot, a simple, intentional interruption to the natural flow of the pattern's spread. "Not a cut," you instruct Anya, "Only a disturbance. Small. Brief. No repetition." She nods, her expression focused, her breath misting in the cold air. She kneels, her fingers hovering over a barely discernible hexagonal imprint in the snow. With exquisite control, a wisp of frost, almost invisible, traces a single, minimal interruption across the pattern. Then, immediately, she stops, her hand still. You all step back, becoming silent observers. The effect is subtle, almost imperceptible at first. The faint hexagonal pattern in the snow, where Anya made her minute disruption, seems to shimmer for a moment, its edges blurring as if struggling to reassert its form. Then, slowly, over the course of several agonizing breaths, the pattern *does* begin to correct itself, but with an observable delay, and the re-forming edges appear fractionally less sharp than before. More strikingly, the crystalline growths nearby, particularly those closest to your marked line, seem to retract almost microscopically, their faint blue luminescence dimming for a heartbeat before returning to normal. From the river, the pulse from the main stones does not react with a dramatic flare, but rather, the rhythmic blue glow seems to skip a beat, a momentary stutter in its steady cadence, before resuming its unwavering pulse. It is a local reaction, subtle but undeniable, a tremor in a vast, silent system. "If this system can be interrupted at the edge," you say, your voice barely a whisper, a cold satisfaction in your tone, "then it can be shaped. If it cannot... then we are standing inside something much larger than we thought." The system has shown its hand; it can be disrupted.
