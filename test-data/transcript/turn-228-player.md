# turn-228 — PLAYER

I do not approach the traveler immediately. "Nothing reaches us without being seen twice," I say quietly. Before any of the tribe steps forward, I turn first to the edge of the settlement, where the land opens toward the path the traveler has taken. I reach outward--not with sight, but with the quiet language I now understand. To the birds that circle above. To the small creatures that cross the snow. To anything that has watched the path before us. "What passed here before this one?" I ask. "Was he alone? Was he followed? Did others fall where he walks?" I listen carefully--not for words alone, but for patterns, fear, repetition. Only once I understand the path behind him do I return my attention to the present. --- I turn to Kael. "No one touches him yet. No one brings him inside." We prepare a place apart from the longhouse: - downwind from the settlement, - with furs and water placed in advance, - with distance enough to protect the tribe, but not abandon him. Compassion does not require carelessness. --- Before stepping forward, I let my form shift subtly. Not into something false--but into something less known. A version of myself that does not immediately reveal who leads this place. I approach with one healer and one watcher only. --- As I near him, I do not begin with questions. I observe first: - his breath, - his skin, - the way he moves, - anything he carries. Any markings, symbols, carvings, or script on his belongings--I study them carefully, letting their meaning reveal itself without assumption. Only then do I speak. "You have reached shelter," I say, calm and measured. "Now tell me--what has been following you that you could not outrun?" --- I do not ask what he wants. I ask what is behind him. And I watch closely: his words, his hesitation, and whether his answer matches what the land has already told me.
