# turn-336 — DM

You do not answer Maelis in front of the others. Rising slowly, you adjust Rune in your arms before gently passing her to Lena. Your gaze returns to Maelis, steady and recognizing. "Walk with me." You step beyond the longhouse, not waiting for agreement, into the crisp edge of winter light where the sounds of the Quiet Weave soften into distance. Snow compresses underfoot. For a time, you say nothing, letting the silence settle between you. Then, quietly, "You saw clearly. And you spoke without trying to please me. That matters." You glance at her, not as a leader to a recruit, but as one woman measuring another who has carried weight alone. "In this place, there are already two who lead in ways the world does not expect." You gesture lightly back toward the longhouse. "Lyrawyn. She sees what most cannot. She will become something like a sage, though she does not yet know the word. And me. I build. Not just structures, not just plans. I build what holds people together when everything else fails." You let that settle, then continue, more quietly, more honestly. "But the hunt... and war... have remained with the men." You look out across the boundary fields, where the patterns lie beneath the snow. "Not because women cannot. Because no one chose to change it. I am choosing to change it." There is no force in the statement, only certainty. "We have learned something here. Every time we allowed difference--true difference--we became stronger. Not weaker. Not divided. Stronger. Not everyone must do everything. But no one should be forbidden from what they could become." A small step closer aligns you with her. "You asked how women like you can become part of this. Not by fitting into what exists. By shaping what does not yet exist." You let the wind pass between you. "Gorok came as you did. He tested. He resisted. He saw what held. He brought us strength we did not have. Not better. Not worse. Different. Necessary." Your voice lowers. "You are the same kind of arrival. We do not have what you are. And if we try to turn you into what we already are... we lose it." Then, more gently, "If women learn the hunt... the fight... the same way children here learn to build, to see, to think... Then what we create will not depend on one kind of strength surviving. It will endure because it has many." Your eyes hold hers now, direct but not pressing. "You do not need to decide who you are here. You only need to decide if you are willing to shape something that does not yet have a name." You step back slightly. "No one will take your people from you. But if you choose this... what you build here will reach them." You turn, beginning to walk back. "Walk with me again when you have your answer." And you leave her with it--a space only she can choose to fill.
