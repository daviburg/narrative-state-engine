# turn-155 — DM

The loft, once merely a storage space, transforms into your sanctuary with Kael each night. Beneath the heavy furs and the shelter of the longhouse, you draw him close. Your hands, still bearing the faint scent of wild greens, explore new contours, guiding him with a blend of ancient elven sensuality and burgeoning primal need. Kael, initially hesitant before your bold inventiveness, soon responds with an eager, protective passion that matches your own. Each embrace becomes a fervent prayer for new life, a nightly ritual steeped in shared warmth and the potent scent of desire. Weeks turn into a moon's cycle, then another. One morning, a subtle nausea, a familiar shift in your body's rhythm, confirms your hope. Another life begins to stir within you. You press Kael's hand to your belly, a silent testament to the tribe's burgeoning future, your heart thrumming with the quiet hope for a son. During the days, after instructing your new apprentice, Elara, in the intricacies of preparing the fermented greens, your mind turns to the future of the tribe's young. You observe the children, Lyrawyn among them, playing within the longhouse and around the camp. Their education is primarily experiential - learning by watching, doing, and listening to the elders' stories. Hunters teach through example, gatherers through shared tasks, and the shaman through ritual and legend. While effective for survival, you see opportunities for more structured knowledge, a blend of their ancestral wisdom with the insights of your own heritage. You begin to consider how best to introduce new ways of learning, to cultivate not just strong bodies, but sharp minds, preparing the next generation for an even more prosperous future.
