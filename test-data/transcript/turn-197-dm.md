# turn-197 — DM

The mountains loom, their silent, watchful presence growing with each arduous step. On the third evening, as the sun dips below the highest ridge, painting the sky in bruised purples and cold oranges, you signal a halt. Rurik and Boruk quickly establish a small, discreet camp, the crackle of their fire feeling almost sacrilegious in the profound stillness. You leave them to it, stepping beyond the meager circle of warmth, the air immediately turning sharper, thinner. With deliberate movements, you draw a slow, perfect circle in the frost-covered ground with the butt of your staff. Within its confines, you carefully place a shard of ice, impossibly clear, glinting like a fallen star, and a piece of obsidian, dark and heavy, taken from your longhouse hearth. You close your eyes, the frigid air biting at your exposed skin, and call upon the silent star--not for a shield, not for concealment, but for announcement. "We have followed your trail," you murmur into the vast, indifferent darkness, your voice barely a whisper against the wind's low sigh. "We come not as prey nor conqueror. We seek to understand what stirs in these mountains." Then, with a deep breath, you open your senses fully, tearing down the protective walls of your mind, allowing the sterile resonance to find you, to wash over you, if it will. The silence deepens, stretching taut, almost humming with an unseen pressure. Rurik and Boruk stand ready, their weapons undrawn but their stances alert, watching your still form with unwavering focus. The cold purpose of the construct, you know, has found you. The waiting begins.
