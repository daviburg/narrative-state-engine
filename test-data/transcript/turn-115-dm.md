# turn-115 — DM

The choice weighs heavily, yet clearly, upon your heart. The stolen trinket, once a driving force, now feels distant, almost trivial, compared to the warmth of belonging and the burgeoning hope for new life. Your gaze sweeps across the bustling, hardy camp, where every face, every task, speaks of survival and community. You realize that your unique arcane abilities, born of celestial pact, could offer more than just personal power; they could be a vital shield and a helping hand for this resilient people. The tribe's immediate goals are evident in their daily struggles: consistent food, warmth against the relentless cold, protection from the harsh elements and wild beasts, and healing for injuries and illness. Your warlock magic, though different from the shaman's primal communion, could bridge gaps. Perhaps your eldritch blasts could ward off predators or aid in the hunt from a distance. Spells of light could extend the short, dim arctic days, aiding in foraging or travel. Minor enchantments might mend worn tools or reinforce fragile shelters. Even simple cantrips could ease their daily burdens, like conjuring warmth or purifying snow for water. By intertwining your power with their needs, you could not only strengthen the tribe but also forge a deeper, more profound connection with your hunter and your chosen home, allowing your magic to evolve in new, unexpected ways.
