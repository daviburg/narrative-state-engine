# turn-242 — PLAYER

I have seen enough. "This is not fever alone," I say quietly to Kael and the Shaman. "It is a pattern inside him." "No one moves closer. The boundary holds." --- I turn to the man. "I see it," I tell him, steady. "It moves in lines. It spreads with intent." "I will try to interrupt it." "Not to harm you--but to give your body a chance." "You must follow exactly." "Breathe when I say. Hold when I say. Do not fight the heat." "If anything changes--tell me at once." --- I set the field. - The perimeter remains. - The healer stands ready with water, no contact. - The watcher tracks breath and time. The Shaman stands behind me--not to act first, but to witness and steady. --- I draw a simple counter-pattern on a flat stone between us. Not complex. Deliberate. A straight line, then a crossing line. Break, not mimic. --- "Anya," I say. "Same control. Smaller." "Trace only the crossing line. No more." "Stop the moment I say." --- I do not touch the man. I do not push power into him. I anchor the moment. I watch the pattern within him as the frost forms. --- I look for only three things: - Does the internal rhythm stutter, even briefly? - Do the lesions along one path dim, pause, or desynchronize? - Does his breath break its cadence? --- If there is any change, even slight: "Stop," I say immediately. We do not repeat. We observe. --- If there is no change: We do not escalate. We change the angle. Next, I have Anya trace the line parallel to his described path--offset, not overlapping. We test interference, not force. --- If at any point: - his breathing collapses, - the marks flare violently, - or the ground beneath stirs-- we stop. Immediately. No second attempt. --- To the Shaman, quietly: "If this is crafted, it can be disrupted." "If it can be disrupted, it can be slowed." "And if it can be slowed--" I glance to the man. "--it can be beaten." --- We attempt once. Then we decide.
