# turn-131 — DM

The construction now moves upwards, towards the sky. Guided by your vision, the tribe begins to erect the steep-pitched roof, a critical shield against the heavy snows and the biting arctic winds. You oversee the careful layering of wood shingles, teaching what you can through gesture and example, occasionally lending a subtle cantrip to strengthen a joint or ease the fitting of a particularly stubborn piece. The rhythmic tap of hammers on wood fills the air, a steady drumbeat of progress. Beneath the rising eaves, the concept of a loft takes shape. It becomes more than just storage; the men build it with a knowing glance, understanding its dual purpose as a secure, dry space for precious resources and a haven for whispered secrets and stolen moments, a private eyrie overlooking the communal warmth below. Kael, particularly, works on it with a focused intensity, his eyes meeting yours with an intimate promise whenever you pass. Simultaneously, the tribe begins the arduous task of insulating the walls and roof. Hands, young and old, gather vast quantities of moss from beneath snow-laden rocks, mixing it with clay-rich earth to create a thick, insulating plaster. The earthy smell of damp moss and mud fills the air as they meticulously daub the mixture into every crevice, transforming the skeleton of beams into a solid, cocoon-like structure. While the heaviest labor is now delegated, you move among them, your growing belly a constant, gentle weight, offering guidance and subtle enchantments to ensure the seal is perfect. The tribe, in turn, watches over you, ensuring you are never overtaxed, their gratitude for your vision evident in their every careful movement.
