# turn-188 — PLAYER

At first light, I kneel outside the longhouse and extend my awareness into the web of living minds around us. I call gently to the wolf-dog and the birds in the canopy above, asking what they have seen in the past three days -- where the herds move, whether predators stalk unusually close, whether the forest behaves as it should. If the game shifts unnaturally, I press further: what frightened them? What drove prey from their paths? Then I walk the perimeter of our settlement with my sight attuned to arcane currents. I search for disturbances -- lingering enchantments, ancient wards buried beneath soil, unnatural voids in the weave, or trails of magic that pass beyond our territory. If I sense anything older than our tribe, I will follow it cautiously, bringing two hunters and one apprentice -- not to conquer, but to understand. At the same time, I gather the children and apprentices within the longhouse and quietly observe them with arcane sight while they work and play. If dormant energy stirs within any of them - a flicker, a resonance, an echo - I take note. The silent star may not have chosen me alone, but guided me here with purpose to reawaken what was lost. Our people grow. The world must answer that growth.
