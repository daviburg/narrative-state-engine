# turn-293 — PLAYER

I do not remain at the center. If this is to hold-- it must move through others. --- "These are not only directions," I say quietly. "They are things you will see done." --- To Kael, I do not explain further. I walk. --- We move beyond the longhouse, across the thawing ground. I kneel where the soil darkens, where water lingers just beneath the surface. --- "Here," I say. "Not because it grows now." "Because it will continue to grow." --- I press the soil. Show him how it holds shape without crumbling. How water drains--but does not vanish. How the wind passes--but does not strip it bare. --- Then I stand, and walk further. To higher ground. To thinner soil. --- "This feeds the hunt." "Not us." --- "Here we do not plant." "We leave it." "So something else can live." --- I do not name every rule. I let him see the difference. --- "You will choose the next place," I tell him. "And I will say nothing--unless you are wrong." --- --- To Lena: "The children will build," I say. "But they will also learn from those who already shape." --- I gesture to Borin and the crafters. --- "Each child joins a hand that knows." "Not all at once." "Not forever." --- "They rotate." "They observe." "They assist." --- "They do not become crafters." "They become capable." --- Then I kneel and draw a simple design. --- A wedge. A handle. A split edge. --- "A tool that does not strike straight," I explain. "It separates." --- "For wood." "For stone." "For anything that must be opened without breaking." --- "A wedge." --- "Make one." "Test it." "Then improve it only if it fails." --- --- To the Shaman: I do not speak first. I sit beside the fragment. --- Anya joins us. --- We remain in silence. --- Then: "We observe together," I say. "Not as teacher and student." "As those who do not yet understand." --- "One change at a time." --- I shift slightly closer. Then farther. --- Anya watches. The Shaman listens. --- "No reaching," I remind her quietly. "Only noticing." --- "If it reacts--" "We record." "If it does not--" "We record." --- "This is how we learn something that does not speak." --- --- To the healer: Each evening, I sit with prepared bark and charcoal. --- "We do not keep this in memory alone," I say. "We place it where others can find it." --- I begin recording: - which plants strengthen - which restore - which fail - and when --- "Not stories," I say. "Patterns." --- "One page each night." "No more." "No less." --- "What we write must be usable--" "without us." --- --- To Borin: I do not describe the structure again. I build a small version beside him. --- Layered walls. Air gaps. Raised floor. Separated storage. --- "Heat rises," I say simply. "Moisture settles." "Air must move--but not freely." --- I show him: - how to vent without losing heat - how to keep food from touching ground - how to separate spaces without sealing them --- "You will not copy this." "You will understand it." --- "Then you build your version." --- "If it holds--" "It becomes ours." --- --- To the southern elders: "You will not only observe the method," I say. "You will observe how we learn." --- I assign one of their shamans to each path: - one to Kael - one to Lena and the crafters - one to the Shaman - one to the healer --- "You do not interfere." "You watch." "You remember." --- Then, after a pause: --- "You will return to your people." --- "And when you come back--" --- "You bring one thing." --- "Not many." "Not stories." --- "One piece of knowledge your people hold--" "that we do not." --- "If we are to share what we know--" "We will not be the only ones who give." --- --- Finally-- I step back from all of them. --- "I will not show everything again," I say quietly. --- "What you have seen--" "You now carry." --- "If it changes when I am not there--" "It was never understood." --- "This place does not grow because I lead it." --- "It grows because what we do here--" "can be done again." --- I do not remain with any one group. --- I move between them. Watching. Correcting only when needed. --- Letting the knowledge take root-- where it will hold
