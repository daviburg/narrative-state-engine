# turn-141 — DM

The pangs grow in intensity, a primal rhythm echoing the very pulse of the earth. You surrender to the powerful force of nature taking hold of your body, your carefully laid plans now mere whispers against the roaring tide of sensation. Kael's hand is a steady anchor, the midwife's voice a low, comforting hum. In the throes of it all, your mind reaches out, not to the warmth of the longhouse, but to the cold, distant light of your silent star. You pour your will, your deepest hope, and a fraction of your pact-bound power into that celestial connection, a silent plea for arcane blessing upon the life struggling to emerge. Hours pass in a blur of effort and ancient instinct. Then, with a final, searing push, a cry pierces the air - sharp, demanding, utterly new. The midwife swaddles a tiny form, and Kael, tears tracing paths through the dirt on his cheeks, reveals a small, perfect face. It is a girl. Her skin is fair, like yours, but with eyes that hold a startling depth, a fleeting glimmer of something ancient and knowing. She is Lyrawyn, a child of two worlds, blessed by the silent star and born in the heart of the arctic wild. Exhaustion washes over you, but also a profound, undeniable sense of completion, and the faint hum of arcane energy, a silent promise, resonates from the tiny bundle in your arms.
