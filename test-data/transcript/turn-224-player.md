# turn-224 — PLAYER

Winter holds, but we do not spend it chasing change that will not come. We maintain what we have built. The sheltered crops are preserved and watched, not expanded further. The fragment remains under observation, its rhythm recorded but not tested. Anya continues her daily practice, repeating what she has mastered, no more. Hunting remains efficient and disciplined, avoiding unnecessary risk. Contact with the southern tribes is maintained, but not pressed. We do not push the world in the deepest cold. We wait for it to move. Days pass. Then more. The cold loosens slightly, then returns, then loosens again. We watch for one thing only: the first true shift. The first sign that winter is no longer tightening, but beginning to release. The first change in the rivers, the air, the animals, or the snow that does not reverse. When that moment comes, we act immediately. I gather the council as soon as the first real sign of thaw appears. We prepare to move again: - to follow the rivers once they begin to break, - to seek the trapper's route and the rumor of glowing stones, - to reassess the southern tribes as movement returns, - and to decide whether to approach the mountain again with what we have learned. I also observe the fragment and Anya closely at that transition: does the changing season alter the fragment's behavior, and does her control shift with it? We do not rush the thaw. But when it comes, we are ready for it. The moment the thaw begins, something from beyond our immediate lands reaches us for the first time since winter began.
