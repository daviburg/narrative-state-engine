# turn-269 — PLAYER

We do not step into the river. We do not touch the stones. We make the place speak without disturbing it. --- I mark a boundary for our camp well above the bank, outside the nearest pattern. "No one crosses to the water without me," I say. --- We begin with distance. Rurik and the tracker circle wide, keeping the river bend in sight from two angles. I want: - the full extent of where animals avoid, - how far the hexagonal melt patterns reach, - and whether the crystalline growths form a line, a ring, or a path. They mark it in the ground as they go. --- At the bank, I observe without crossing. I watch the stones: - their pulse timing, - whether they align with each other or stagger, - whether the rhythm matches the fragment or differs. I count cycles. I compare. --- We test without contact. Rurik casts a loose stone from dry ground toward the edge of the pulsing cluster--short, controlled, not striking directly. We watch: - does the pulse change, - does the water react, - does anything answer? --- I set two markers: - one upstream of the bend, - one downstream. We watch the water between them. Does anything carried by the current change as it passes the stones? Foam, ice, debris--anything. --- I use arcane sight--but only at range. I do not reach into the stones. I look for: - whether the same structured pattern as the sickness exists here, - whether the flow follows the river or crosses it, - and whether anything connects the stones to the distant mountain. --- No collection. No extraction. Not yet. --- We end the day with a map. Not of distance-- of influence. Where it begins. Where it strengthens. Where it fades. --- If this place spreads the pattern, it will reveal how. If it answers, it will answer in change. Show me what shifts when we observe it without touching it.
