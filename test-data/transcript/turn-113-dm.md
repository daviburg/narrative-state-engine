# turn-113 — DM

The days bleed into weeks, each one a rhythmic cycle of communal tasks and quiet intimacy. You rise with the tribe, contributing to whatever labor presents itself--mending furs alongside the women, aiding in the preparation of meals, and occasionally joining the less strenuous gathering expeditions. By day, you are a valued, if still somewhat enigmatic, member of the community. By night, you return to the warmth of your hunter's furs, his silent embrace becoming a familiar comfort, a language spoken without words. Each morning, as you stir from sleep, a hopeful glance falls upon your belly, a private vigil for the subtle signs of new life taking root. The cold, stark beauty of the arctic becomes a familiar backdrop to this new chapter, yet a small part of you still recalls the stolen item and the purpose that first brought you to this frozen land, a reminder of the world beyond the tribe's reach.
