# turn-045 — DM

You join the small gathering around the main bonfire, finding a space offered to you near the women you'd been helping. The air is thick with the aroma of the stew, a hearty mix of wild game and root vegetables. Bowls are passed around, and you receive a generous portion, the warmth seeping into your hands. As you eat, you subtly observe the villagers. There's a clear hierarchy, with the elder at the head, but also a strong sense of community and shared hardship. Whispered conversations and knowing glances pass between them, conveying information without words. You notice that no one has attempted to disarm you, your staff still resting securely at your side, its presence seemingly overlooked or deliberately ignored. Perhaps the initial capture was more about assessing a potential threat than outright robbery. The meal is eaten in relative silence, punctuated only by the crackling fire and the slurping of broth, a quiet ritual that speaks volumes about their way of life.
