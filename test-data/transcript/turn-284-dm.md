# turn-284 — DM

"We have what we came for," you declare quietly, your gaze lingering one last time on the river stones, their pulse now a weak, flickering echo of its former strength. "We do not press further--not because we cannot, but because we choose where to act." You look to Rurik and the others. "We leave no trace. The disruptions remain--but nothing more." With a final, silent nod, you begin your withdrawal, retracing your steps along the trapper's path. The movement is steady, purposeful, not hastened by fear, nor slowed by hesitation, but vigilant for any change in the disrupted landscape behind you. As you move, the geometry, the precise spacing of the triangular fields, and the system's observable responses are committed not just to memory, but to a deeper understanding, solidifying into a fully formed method. "The river is not our enemy," you murmur, "It is a machine. And now we know how it fails." Your return to the longhouse is not for reporting, but for applying. The knowledge gleaned from the river stones, the precise understanding of how to disrupt and weaken the alien pattern, now has a direct purpose. "The next step is not here," you conclude, your gaze sweeping across the familiar, sturdy walls of your home. "It is where our people live. And where this knowledge will matter." The journey east has yielded a profound tool, one you are now ready to wield to protect your own.
