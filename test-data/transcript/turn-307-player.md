# turn-307 — PLAYER

I do not rise immediately. I do not give orders. I do not allow celebration to define this moment. I remain still, holding Rune, observing--not as mother alone, but as one who must understand what has entered the world. "What arrived is not only a child," I say quietly. "It is alignment." I look first to the healer. "Is there anything in her body that does not follow natural form?" Then to the Shaman. "Does her presence alter the fragment--or only resonate with it?" Then to Anya. "Do you feel her... or does she feel like the pattern itself?" Then to Lyrawyn. "Does the song change near her--or does it become easier?" I do not rush their answers. I listen for consistency. Not wonder. Not fear. Truth. Then I look to Kael. "No one approaches her without purpose." "This is not protection from harm." "It is protection from influence." I turn back to the council. "We defined balance." "Now we test if it holds." I establish three immediate conditions. Rune is never left within a disruption field. Rune is never brought near the fragment for extended time. Rune is always observed in both structured and unstructured spaces. "We do not raise her in only one world," I state. "She must exist in both." Then, more quietly: "If she stabilizes everything--she becomes a single point of failure." "If she disrupts nothing--she may erase what we preserved." I pause. Then define her role--not as child, but as function. "She is not the center of the Quiet Weave." "She is a thread within it." I look to Lena. "She learns as the others do." No exception. No acceleration. No isolation. Then finally-- I look down at her. And I listen. Not to what she is. But to what changes around her-- without anyone acting.
