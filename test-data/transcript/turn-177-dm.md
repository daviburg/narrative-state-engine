# turn-177 — DM

Your practical mind identifies improved pottery as the most impactful next step for the tribe's everyday life. Durable, waterproof vessels would revolutionize food storage, cooking, and even water collection, especially in the freezing climate. You decide to begin with a small, experimental kiln, something you can manage yourself during these final months of your pregnancy. You instruct Kael and a few hunters on the specific type of clay you'll need - a finer, less stony consistency than what they currently use for crude drying. While they gather the raw material, you meticulously select a sheltered, well-ventilated spot a safe distance from the longhouse. Gathering suitable firewood is a critical step, requiring seasoned, dry branches that will burn hot and long enough. Day by day, your belly growing more prominent, you supervise the construction: a simple, beehive-shaped structure of unfired clay and rocks, designed to hold and radiate heat. Once the small kiln is ready, you mold a few basic, practical vessels - a sturdy bowl, a small storage jar - shaping the clay with deliberate, careful hands. The firing is a tense, smoky process, requiring constant monitoring of the heat. When the kiln finally cools and you crack it open, the smooth, hardened ceramic of your first creations gleams faintly, a clear demonstration of new possibilities. The tribe gathers, marveling at the strength and impermeability of the finished pieces, their faces alight with understanding of the profound value this innovation brings. This initial success sets the stage for a larger, communal effort, a grander kiln to meet the growing needs of your thriving tribe.
