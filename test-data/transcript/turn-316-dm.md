# turn-316 — DM

You do not answer all at once, your gaze deliberate as it sweeps over the assembled leaders. You turn first to Chief Thorne, his wary eyes still assessing every detail. "You do not risk belief," you state, your voice calm. "You risk nothing you cannot afford to lose." Two of your people step forward, bearing sealed bundles of dried meat, preserved tubers, and fermented stores, placing them before the Chief. "This is enough for your people to pass the winter without starvation." You let the weight of the offer settle. "You take this with no promise in return. Not as trade. As proof." You meet his gaze, unwavering. "If this were luck, it would not be given away." Then, softening your tone just enough to invite, "You do not commit your people. You send two. They learn. They return. If it fails, you lose only time. If it holds, your people do not starve again." Thorne's jaw loosens almost imperceptibly, his initial suspicion giving way to a flicker of profound relief, mixed with a calculating assessment of the offer. Then, your attention shifts to Elder Lyra. "You do not ask about food. You ask about harmony." You motion for her to walk with you, leading her not far, but to a spot overlooking the cultivated ground, the moss borders, and the children learning, Lyrawyn among them, effortlessly tracing patterns in the earth. "The land was not forced," you explain, kneeling and placing your hand lightly on the soil, "It was listened to. The sickness broke balance. We did not fight the land. We restored its ability to respond." You rise, facing her. "You will be given our records. Not simplified. Not altered. Everything we observed. Everything we tested. Including what failed. And you will see the healing done again, with your own people, under your own eyes. If your shamans seek spirits, let them watch what returns when nothing is forced." Lyra's gaze brightens, a deep, ancestral yearning for balance reflected in her wise eyes. She nods slowly, a gesture of profound respect. Finally, you turn to Warrior-Chief Gorok, his skepticism still a palpable force. You do not dismiss him, but acknowledge his truth. "You are correct," you say plainly, without pause or softening. "What we have built feeds many. It does not yet withstand many who would take it." You let the stark reality stand, undefended. "We have hunters. We do not yet have warriors. And because of what we have created... we will be seen." You step closer, not as a challenge, but as one speaking to an equal, understanding his language of strength. "You speak of strength. So do I." You gesture, not to the fields, but to your people, to Kael standing silently watchful nearby. "I chose the strongest hunter of this tribe to stand beside me. Not for comfort. For continuity. I do not build with weakness." Your voice hardens slightly. "You bring something we do not have. The art of war. Discipline under threat. The knowledge of how men break--and how they hold." You turn slightly, allowing him to see the settlement's abundance. "We bring something you do not have. Food that does not fail. Stability that does not collapse. Knowledge that compounds." You return your full attention to him, your words a precise, unyielding structure. "Separate, we survive. Together, we become something others cannot challenge. You do not come here to become us. You come here to add what we lack. Your warriors train here. They shape how this place defends itself. In return, your people do not starve. Your strength is sustained--not consumed. And when others come to take what we have built... They will meet both." You step back, letting the silence hang heavy, a choice laid bare. "This is not peace instead of strength. It is strength that does not erode itself." Gorok's scowl deepens, but his eyes, rather than dismissing, now pierce through the surface, assessing the stark, undeniable logic of your offer. He fears losing his people's identity, yet covets the strength and abundance you embody.
