# turn-125 — DM

The vision for a sturdier, warmer home for your child, and for the entire tribe, takes root in your mind. You begin to observe the existing structures with new eyes, mentally dissecting their weaknesses and envisioning improvements. Your days now become a careful balance: you still aid in the tribe's daily rhythm, but a significant portion of your time is dedicated to this grander design. You start by seeking out the shaman, using gestures and crude drawings in the snow to convey your ideas for a larger, more permanent dwelling with a raised floor, protected from the cold earth. He listens intently, his brow furrowed, then points to specific areas, indicating suitable ground and potential sources for the dense, snow-covered timber and resilient stones needed for corner foundations. The sheer scale of the undertaking is daunting, requiring not only your arcane touch but many hands. Gathering the raw materials in this harsh environment alone is a monumental task, but your resolve is unwavering, fueled by the image of a warm, safe haven for your growing family and adopted kin.
