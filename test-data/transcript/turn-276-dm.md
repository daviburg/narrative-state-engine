# turn-276 — DM

"One flaw forces attention," you observe quietly, your gaze flicking from the subtly struggling pattern to the untouched expanse of its influence. "Two may force a choice." You leave the first disruption precisely as it is, an enduring, irritating imperfection. You select a second point along the boundary, far enough from the first that the system cannot treat them as a single anomaly. It is a different section of the influence, where the hexagonal melt patterns are just as tentative, the crystalline growth equally sparse. "The same as before," you instruct Anya. "No more. No less. Only once." Her movements are fluid, almost ethereal, as she introduces a second faint disruption, a whisper of frost upon the thawing ground. Then, her hand stills completely. The Shaman's Echo-Song continues its low, persistent hum, a steady pressure against the pervasive, alien coherence. You step back, your gaze now encompassing both points of interference, observing them not just locally, but as a combined force. Both disruptions remain stable, small pockets of imperfection that the larger system struggles to smooth over. Each continues to resist the pattern's full reformation, the edges of the hexagonal melts and crystalline growths around them stubbornly refusing to fully reassert their geometric precision. It is clear that the system is dedicating resources to each, dividing its correction across two separate wounds. The river stones, however, tell a more profound story. The pulse, while still emanating a blue light, now stutters more frequently, not just an occasional missed beat, but a noticeable desynchronization, a slight wavering in the overall rhythm. The intensity of the glow, too, seems to diminish subtly, as if the energy that once flowed so smoothly is now fractionated, struggling to maintain its equilibrium. It is not failing, not yet, but it is demonstrably under increased strain, its unwavering perfection now marred by two persistent irritations. You do nothing further, no third point, no adjustment. "If it must choose where to be perfect," you whisper, "then we decide where it cannot be." The system, once a symbol of impenetrable order, is now revealed to be capable of distraction, forced to divide its flawless attention.
