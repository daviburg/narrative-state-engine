# turn-147 — DM

The mead making becomes a dedicated, almost ritualistic task in the quiet months. You carefully monitor the fermentation, ensuring the precious honey is not wasted. Your heightened senses, attuned to subtle energies, help you gauge the health of the brew, nudging the process with gentle warmth or cooling. The initial batches are small, tasted with caution and shared only with Kael, who, after initial suspicion, finds himself surprisingly fond of the sweet, potent drink. Eventually, you perfect the process, creating a golden, amber liquid that offers not just warmth but a gentle, intoxicating comfort--a rare luxury in the harsh arctic. Once confident in its safety and consistency, you approach the shaman, offering him a small, ceremonial cup. His eyes widen in surprise as he tastes it, a slow smile spreading across his grizzled face. He nods his approval. You then begin to seek out an apprentice. A young woman, observant and quick, who often helped with gathering, shows particular interest. Patiently, through gestures and the few shared words, you begin to unravel the secrets of fermentation, teaching her the art of transforming nature's bounty into liquid warmth, further cementing your place as a bringer of new knowledge and comfort to the tribe.
