# turn-165 — DM

The progress you've spurred within the tribe fills you with a deep satisfaction, yet your mind, ever restless, now turns outward. The longhouse, though robustly built, still rests within the "crude, hidden encampment" where you first found yourself. Its strength lies in its remoteness and the tribe's cunning ability to blend with the landscape. While its thick walls offer significant protection against the elements and even a casual intrusion, a determined band of raiders, if they somehow discovered your location, could pose a serious threat. Kael and the hunters have established more systematic patrols, and the elder's keen eyes remain ever watchful for unfamiliar tracks in the snow, but the longhouse itself has no formalized defenses beyond its sturdy construction and the tribe's collective vigilance. As for other tribes, the arctic is a vast, unforgiving expanse, and settlements are few and far between. The elder speaks of distant, nomadic groups - some known for their fierce independence and territoriality, others for their quiet reclusiveness. Trade is rare, often limited to specific, highly valued resources like rare furs or mineral salts, typically occurring at designated, neutral meeting points far from any permanent settlement. The concept of taking in large numbers of refugees is fraught with peril in this resource-scarce environment; any additional mouths to feed strain the delicate balance the tribe maintains. Small, isolated individuals might find sanctuary, but an entire displaced group would present a challenge bordering on catastrophic. Your tribe values its self-sufficiency above all else, a hard-won trait in this harsh land.
