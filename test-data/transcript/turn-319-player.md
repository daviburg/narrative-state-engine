# turn-319 — PLAYER

I do not celebrate their answers. I set them in motion. For Thorne, I act quietly. The two young trackers are received without ceremony. They are fed, housed, and observed. Two of our maidens are assigned to them--not as guards, not as servants, but as constant presence. They guide them through the work by day: fields, paths, storage, tracking. By night, they do not leave them alone. No force. No instruction. Only proximity, warmth, and continuity. Youth, beauty and nature will ensure the rest. I watch not for obedience, but for shift--how quickly they begin to speak as we do, decide as we do, and whether they begin to imagine returning... or remaining. If they want to remain, they will be deeply motivated to convince their tribe to unite. I require the outcome to be shown clearly over time: whether they integrate, resist, or attempt to reshape what they learn. For Lyra, I do not hold knowledge back. She is given full access to the records, the healing, and the observation sites. But more importantly, I create a deliberate pairing. Lyrawyn walks with her. Not as student alone, and not as child to be protected, but as one who already perceives. They observe together, speak together, and share what each senses that the other does not. I want to see whether Lyra refines Lyrawyn--or whether Lyrawyn changes Lyra. I require the outcome: what new knowledge emerges between them that neither possessed alone. For Gorok, I define the trial. He is not allowed to test randomly. The test is structured. Three phases. Three nights. First: boundary. His warriors attempt to approach, probe, and bypass the outer defensive lines and disruption fields without being detected or redirected. Second: pressure. A simulated raid at night against the edge of the settlement, with our people defending using current methods only. No lethal force. The goal is control, not destruction. Third: mind. A war game. No weapons. Only positioning, deception, and prediction. Gorok commands his side. I command ours. Kael observes, not leads. Before it begins, I state one thing clearly. "If you test only strength, you will miss how this place wins." I also prepare. I do not rely on Kael's experience alone. I apply what I know. Do not meet force where it is strongest. Shape where it arrives. Control movement. Limit options. Force decisions before contact. I want the outcome shown in detail: where our system fails, where it holds, and whether Gorok adapts--or dismisses. I do not interfere once it begins. I observe all three paths in parallel. And I expect time to pass--days, not moments--until each path produces a clear result.
