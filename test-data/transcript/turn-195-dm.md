# turn-195 — DM

The stark vision leaves an undeniable chill in your bones, but also a crystal-clear purpose. You return to the longhouse, your steps firm despite the tremor still running through your arcane senses. Kael, Lena, the Shaman, and the senior hunters gather at your summons, their expressions expectant. "What lies east," you begin, your voice low and resonant, "is not a tribe nor a beast. It is an ancient construct of cold purpose. If we approach it, we do so with order, not impulse." You lay out your plan, the gravity of your words settling like a heavy cloak over the hushed assembly. Kael's jaw tightens as you explain his vital role: "You will not accompany me. If I do not return, the tribe must not fracture. You remain as acting leader of hunts and defender of the settlement. Lena continues instruction. The Shaman guides spiritual matters. This order stands until my return or fifteen days beyond my departure." His eyes, though pained, meet yours in understanding, a silent promise exchanged. "Rurik," you continue, turning to the stoic tracker, "your most trusted, will travel with me to protect me." Another hunter, known for his relentless endurance, is chosen to complete the small party. The next seven days are a whirlwind of quiet, focused activity. Every weapon in the tribe is inventoried; bowstrings are reinforced, arrows fletched, and two sturdy spears, crafted for the rugged mountain journey, are prepared. Rations for ten days, painstakingly dried and packed, are assembled. Fur-lined cloaks, impervious to the highest winds, are sewn with deliberate stitches. Charms against cold and, more subtly, against unnatural influences, are gathered from the Shaman's stores. Every tool, every piece of gear, is assessed, leaving nothing to chance. Before departure, at the longhouse threshold, you kneel. With focused intent, you weave a subtle tether of perception through your pact, extending your senses like invisible tendrils. Now, if arcane energy comparable to the sterile trail approaches within a bowshot of the settlement, you will feel it, no matter how far you travel. Night watches are doubled for the first three days of your absence, then resume their standard rotation. The sound traps on the eastern perimeter are checked, tightened, and reinforced. Faelan remains under Lena's careful gaze and the protection of two elder mothers, while Lyrawyn is instructed not to leave the inner ring of the settlement until your return. The tribe, though somber, does not feel abandoned; they feel entrusted, empowered to maintain the home you've built. Anya walks with you alone, her young face a mixture of curiosity and concern. "You will not seek power while I am gone," you tell her gently. "You will listen." You instruct her to sit at the eastern boundary each dawn, observing the fading arcane residue without touching it, recording her impressions in symbols. She is to speak with animals, but not to impose her will. She must inform the Shaman immediately if her dreams intensify, or if her spark flares unbidden, and, most critically, to alert Kael and the Shaman at once if she feels the sterile presence draw nearer. On the eighth dawn, under a sky still painted in hues of deep violet and emerging grey, you depart. Rurik, a silent sentinel, and the chosen hunter, whose name is Boruk, move with practiced ease. You travel light, but prepared, your mission clear: confirm terrain, gauge the structure's scale, identify any presence, and withdraw if directly challenged. "The insulation of our tribe ends here," you say quietly to Kael, your hand briefly clasping his before you turn eastward, towards the jagged, foreboding peaks. "Not through conquest, but through knowledge."
