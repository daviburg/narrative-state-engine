# turn-328 — DM

"A woman to match his fire," you muse thoughtfully, watching Kael's face for his reaction. "You are precisely right, Kael. You know these lands and these people since your birth, better than I ever could. Please, go find such a woman, or maybe a few, and bring them back here to our settlement. Not just so we may find one to continue Gorok's mountain of strength, but also so I may form something else uniquely new and powerful." You pause, letting the implications sink in. "I once read about a tribe of all-female warriors whom none could match how fierce they are. Their cool in battle, their strategic mind, their unity, that would be a powerful asset to our tribe. What say you?" Kael's brow furrows deeper, his hand dropping from your cheek. His gaze, which had been so clear, now clouds with a complex mix of surprise, ingrained tradition, and a deep, almost instinctual protectiveness. The idea of women, beyond the hunt, as a distinct fighting force, seems to challenge a fundamental understanding of his world. His people's women are strong, resilient, essential to the tribe's survival, but their strength is in nurturing, in enduring, in the quiet, foundational work of life. Warriors are men. "All-female warriors..." he murmurs, the words sounding foreign on his tongue, almost a question. He looks from your determined expression to the distant, snow-covered peaks, then back to the subtle patterns of the Quiet Weave around the settlement. He trusts your vision, your profound insights into the patterns of life and strength, but this is a pattern he has never observed, never even conceived. A long silence stretches between you, punctuated only by the crisp whisper of the wind. Finally, he turns, his eyes meeting yours, a fierce loyalty overriding his traditional reservations. "Such women, if they exist," he begins slowly, "would be... formidable. They would be as unexpected as your patterns, Fenouille." He looks towards the southern horizon, where the other tribes dwell. "It would not be easy. They would have to be chosen with great care. Not all spirits welcome new paths. But," he adds, his jaw firming, "if this brings strength, enduring strength, to our children, to *our* tribe... I will seek them. I will speak your words, and I will bring them to you. For our children, Fenouille, I will do what must be done." His acceptance is not immediate enthusiasm, but a profound commitment, born of his unwavering faith in your purpose and his absolute devotion to the future of his family and tribe.
