# turn-212 — PLAYER

Autumn does not linger forever. Preparation without testing is only theory. Three weeks pass under steady labor. The wind grows sharper. The light shortens. I call a council within the longhouse -- not to plan, but to assess what has changed. The Crops We inspect the covered growing patch. I do not ask whether it worked. I examine what survived and what failed. If even one plant endured the first hard frost, I mark it for propagation. If all failed, I adapt the design immediately. I ask Borin: "What did the kiln teach us? What cracked? What held?" No experiment ends without conclusion. The Southern Tribes I do not wait for them to approach. The gift-bearing hunters return -- and they return with an answer. I ask: Did the southern elders accept diplomacy? Did they reject it? Did they demand something in return? Did they warn us of pressures further south? Neutral space breeds decision. Silence does not. The Trapper If rivers carry glowing stones, then the rivers have spoken by now. Scouts have checked the nearest waterways twice since my return. I ask plainly: Have any strange stones surfaced? Have any outsiders passed through? Has sickness crept northward? Rumor either strengthens or dissolves with time. The Fragment Three weeks of observation. That is enough for pattern. I compare the fragment's pulse across recorded nights. Does it align with mountain rhythms? Has the boundary shifted measurably since my return? If the mountain breathes, we now know its cycle. Anya Three weeks of listening. I ask her to bring her drawings forward. If her dreams intensified, describe the strongest image. If they faded, that too is knowledge. If the spark has grown, I begin controlled testing: A simple exercise -- can she alter frost along a straight line no wider than a finger? Power must be measurable. The Tribe Scarcity does not wait for spring. I ask Kael: Have hunting returns improved or declined under the new pattern? Are tensions with the southern tribes rising or easing? Has any sickness shown among our people? Preparation only matters if it changes outcomes. Then I say quietly to the gathered council: "We have worked long enough. The world has had time to answer."
