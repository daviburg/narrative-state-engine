# turn-234 — PLAYER

I listen to his answers without interruption. Fever. Then breath. Then marks. No recovery. That is the pattern. But patterns are not truths until tested. --- I turn to the healer. "We do not try to save him by force." "We give his body the chance to fight." --- I begin to define it clearly, so there is no misunderstanding. "He is not to be made stronger quickly." "He is to be made steady." --- I instruct: - warm furs, but not excessive heat, - small amounts of water, regularly, not in bursts, - crushed roots only after we identify them--nothing unknown is given yet, - no direct contact--tools only. "We observe whether the fever rises or breaks." "If the body is fighting, we support it." "If it is failing, we learn how." --- I kneel again, but at distance, studying him with full attention. "Your body is burning," I tell the man. "Do not fight that." "Tell me--when the fever rose in others... did it ever fall before they died?" --- I turn briefly to the healer. "We mark time." "How long between stages matters more than the stages themselves." --- Then I stand, and my voice changes. Harder now. For the tribe. --- "If he dies, nothing remains." "No burial." "No leaving to the ground." "No delay." --- I look directly at Kael. "We burn the body completely." "Not until it blackens--until it is gone." "The fire is watched. The remains are not touched." "What is left is scattered where nothing grows and nothing drinks." --- I gesture to the ground where he lies. "This place is not used again." "Snow, ash, and time will take it." "No one walks here without purpose." --- Then, quieter again: "If the earth takes the bodies..." "We do not give it more." --- I look once more at the man. Not as a victim. As a test. --- "We will see," I say calmly, "whether this ends the same way--or not."
