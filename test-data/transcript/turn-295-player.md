# turn-295 — PLAYER

We do not rush the work. We repeat it. Day after day. --- The land softens. Snow gives way to water. Water gives way to soil. --- I do not change the method. I let it continue. --- Kael walks the land without me. Lena rotates the children. Borin builds and rebuilds. The healer records each night. The Shaman and Anya observe the fragment in stillness. The southern shamans watch, then begin to mirror what they have seen. --- I move between them. Less each day. --- Correcting only when something fails to hold. --- Time passes. Not in moments-- but in cycles. --- From thaw... to growth. From early spring... toward the first signs of summer. --- The ground no longer resists every step. Green begins to hold. Animals no longer pass through-- they remain. --- Only then-- I gather them again. --- "We have repeated this long enough," I say quietly. "Now we measure what remains when I am not there." --- I do not ask what was done. I ask what endured. --- To Kael: "Show me the land you chose." "What grows where you planted?" "What failed--and why?" "Where did the hunt hold--and where did it thin?" --- To Lena: "Which children can now teach what they learned?" "Which ones only repeat--and which ones understand?" "What did they build that still stands?" --- To Borin: "Show me the structure." "Not how it was made--" "but how it holds." "Where does it fail under time--not effort?" --- To the healer: "Bring what was written." "What patterns repeat across days?" "What strengthens--and what does not?" --- To the Shaman and Anya: "What changed in the fragment?" "Not once--but over time." "What reacts consistently?" "What does not change at all?" --- To the southern shamans: "What have you understood without being told again?" "What did you attempt on your own?" "What failed?" --- I pause. --- Then, more quietly: --- "What changed... that we did not intend?" --- I step outside. --- I look at the land. Not as it was-- but as it is now. --- Where growth holds. Where it does not. Where paths have formed without being planned. Where animals return--or avoid. --- "We do not measure effort," I say. "We measure what remains." --- I let the silence stretch. I lay my hands on my growing belly and I hope for a kick, a peaceful smile on my face. --- "Now show me what this place has become."
