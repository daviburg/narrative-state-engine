# turn-189 — DM

At the first blush of dawn, before the world has fully shaken off its slumber, you step outside the longhouse. The crisp arctic air fills your lungs as you kneel, allowing your mind to unfurl, reaching into the silent, intricate web of life around your home. You call gently, not with voice, but with intent, to the wolf-dog stirring at the entrance and to the ravens perched like obsidian sentinels in the skeletal branches overhead. Their responses drift into your awareness, a mosaic of instinct and simple observation. The wolf-dog, accustomed to your presence, conveys a general sense of unease, a heightened watchfulness for the past three days. It has marked the increasing movement of the great caribou herds to the north and west, far more active than usual for this early spring. Predators, it suggests, are following. More significantly, it carries a faint scent of *strangers*--not the familiar human scent of your tribe, nor the musk of local animals, but something else, carried on the wind from the east, faint but persistent. The ravens, from their vantage, confirm the herd movements and the general return of smaller prey to the thawing lands. They speak of the unsettling, bright flickers in the distant eastern mountains, not just three days ago, but intermittently throughout the fading winter, and a vague, collective avoidance of that direction. With this new intelligence humming in your mind, you begin your slow walk of the perimeter. Your arcane sight, now sharpened, pierces the mundane veil. The cleared ground, the placed sound traps - they appear as they are. But as you pass the eastern edge, where the snow-laden pines begin to thicken once more, you detect it: a faint, dissipating shimmer in the air, a residual energy that feels alien to the land. It's not a ward, nor an ancient inscription, but a *trail*, a faint, almost invisible pathway of magical discharge that seems to fade into the direction of the distant, light-flashing mountains. It's old enough to be dissolving, but recent enough to leave an impression. It feels cold, sterile, unlike the vibrant energies you've come to associate with the Feywild or the Shaman's earthy power. Returning to the longhouse, you gather the children and apprentices. As they learn and play, their innocent energies a bright counterpoint to the wild outside, you cast your arcane gaze over them. Lyrawyn glows with a nascent, ethereal light, just as you sensed before. Faelan, though still an infant, pulses with a strong, fundamental life force, rich with potential, but as yet undefined by magic. Among the older children, while most are pure and clear of arcane energy, a quiet girl named Anya, one of Lena's brightest students, shows a faint, almost imperceptible *spark* of resonance - a gentle warmth, like a tiny ember, deep within her spirit. It is not overt, but it is there, a subtle echo of the silent star's reach. Your people grow, Fenouille, and the world indeed begins to answer.
