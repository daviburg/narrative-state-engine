# turn-149 — DM

Months continue to weave their tapestry around you and Lyrawyn. Your apprentice, a bright and eager young woman named Tala, has become adept at the mead-making process. She now tends the crocks with confidence, her hands moving with the learned familiarity of a true initiate, freeing you to look to your next endeavor. Your thoughts turn to sustenance for the long winter ahead, a preservation technique that could enrich the tribe's diet: sauerkraut. This requires cabbage, salt, and a press. The salt is a known commodity, used for curing meats, though always a precious resource. Finding a suitable 'cabbage' in this arctic environment, however, presents a unique challenge. With Lyrawyn now a sturdy infant, capable of being bundled safely and left for short periods with Kael or Tala, you feel confident enough to make careful forays outside the longhouse. You choose a day when the winds are low and the sun, though weak, manages to pierce the perpetual twilight. Wrapped in heavy furs, a small digging stick in hand, you venture beyond the immediate camp perimeter, your eyes scanning the snow-dusted landscape for any hardy, leafy greens that might resemble cabbage, knowing that even the most resilient plants hide beneath the frozen crust, waiting for a chance to emerge.
