# turn-167 — DM

Your strategic mind, ever seeking to safeguard your growing family and tribe, now focuses intently on defense. Despite the early stages of your new pregnancy, a fierce resolve steels your spirit, propelling you to action. You begin by meticulously surveying the immediate surroundings of the longhouse, identifying key areas where the dense brush and smaller trees offer too much natural concealment for any potential approach. With Kael at your side, you articulate your vision: a series of layered defenses, starting with a clear perimeter. The first, arduous task is clearing this natural cover. Though your physical strength remains considerable, you wisely prioritize directing the efforts of Kael and the other hunters, pointing out areas to clear. The work is slow and painstaking in the frozen ground, but gradually, lines of sight begin to open up around the longhouse. For the outer perimeter, you begin to design simple but effective sound traps--hollowed bones, dried gourds filled with small pebbles, or taut animal hides stretched over frames--that could be strategically placed to detect any movement beyond the newly cleared zone. The more significant undertakings, a defensive ditch or a basic palisade, remain conceptual for now, a larger undertaking that will require the full commitment of the tribe. Yet, the initial, crucial steps to secure your home have begun, your mind already envisioning the funneling choke points and reinforced defenses that will protect your burgeoning legacy.
