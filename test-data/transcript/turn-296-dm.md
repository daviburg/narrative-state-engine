# turn-296 — DM

"We have repeated this long enough," you declare, your voice cutting through the familiar hum of activity in the Quiet Weave. "Now we measure what remains when I am not there." The council gathers, their faces reflecting a mix of anticipation and newfound confidence. You do not ask what was done, but what endured. Kael steps forward, his gaze sweeping the thawing landscape. "The land we chose, Fenouille, it holds. Where we planted, growth has begun to hold the soil, a surprising resilience in the new shoots. Some small failures, where water pooled too long, taught us to refine our choices. The hunt now follows more predictable paths, the herds shifting around the disruption fields, making our efforts more efficient." Lena recounts the children's progress. "Those like Lyrawyn and a few others now teach what they learned, guiding younger ones through basic patterns in frost and soil. Most can repeat, but a select few truly understand the purpose. They've built small, sturdy windbreaks and shelters for saplings that have held against the spring winds." Borin presents his structure. "It holds, Fenouille, remarkably well. The air gaps prove their worth; no condensation, no rot in the separated storage. The only failure under time was a slight settling of one corner due to uneven ground, easily corrected with a deeper foundation." The healer unrolls his charcoal-marked barks. "The patterns repeat, Fenouille. Certain plants, particularly the mosses and lichens near the warming rocks, strengthen consistently. Others, those in the wetter soils, yield less potency, but never fail entirely. We've begun to cross-reference their locations with the subtle shifts of the disruption fields." The Shaman and Anya approach, their movements calm and precise. "The fragment," the Shaman begins, "has quieted further. Its fluctuations are now predictable, almost rhythmic. No hostile reaction, only a consistent pulse that Anya can now almost predict. It does not change at all in its core, but its aura shifts with a subtle, geometric breath." Anya's frost patterns, you note, are sharper, more effortless. The southern shamans, their elder spokesperson, speaks with humility and pride. "We understood much without being told again, Fenouille. We attempted to place smaller fields near our own hunting grounds. Initial failures, where our intent wavered or our focus broke, taught us much. But now, we have seen success, enough to know the method is true." You pause, letting the collective reports settle. Then, more quietly, "What changed... that we did not intend?" Kael mentions a new, hardier grass thriving unexpectedly within one of the field's edges, Lena notes a peculiar, structured bird's nest woven near the longhouse, the healer a sudden, potent bloom of a rare flower. Stepping outside, you look at the land. It is no longer a hostile expanse, but a canvas taking form. The areas of growth are clear, vibrant where patterns have been introduced. Paths have formed, trodden by focused purpose. You lay your hands on your growing belly, a peaceful smile gracing your lips as a gentle flutter responds within. "Now show me what this place has become."
