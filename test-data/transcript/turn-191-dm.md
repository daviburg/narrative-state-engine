# turn-191 — DM

You rise with the first silver-grey light, the voices of the wild still whispering in your mind. Kael and the other hunters gather, their breath misting in the cold air, their eyes keen and expectant. You convey the new intelligence: the herds are moving, earlier and with an unfamiliar urgency. "From this day," you declare, your voice calm but resolute, "our hunts change. No party travels blind. Two walk ahead as pathfinders, speaking with the land, before arrows are ever loosed. And one remains always near the longhouse, a constant watcher. We hunt with patience now, not only hunger." The hunters, accustomed to your wisdom, nod in understanding, their expressions grim but accepting of the increased vigilance. Later, within the longhouse, you address the wider tribe. "The world shifts, as it always has in spring," you begin, your voice measured, banishing any hint of panic. "But we prepare not for war, but for change. Firewood will be gathered in greater measure. Our watches strengthened. The eastern paths observed, for now." A murmur of understanding ripples through the gathered faces, a subtle reinforcement of communal resolve. That afternoon, you seek out Anya. "Come, walk with me," you suggest, and the quiet girl, her eyes wide with a mixture of awe and curiosity, follows you beyond the immediate camp. You speak of dreams and unnamed feelings, not in abstract terms, but by drawing her attention to the world around them. "Listen," you instruct, gesturing to the wind soughing through the skeletal trees. "To the animals. To the quiet spaces between your own thoughts." You teach her to *feel* the world, to sense its subtle energies without trying to grasp or control them, laying the groundwork for a future that might include the awakening of her own spark. Each dawn you return to the eastern boundary, your arcane sight reaching out like a delicate hand. The faint, sterile trail of magic is still there, a fleeting imprint on the frigid air. You trace its rhythm, its gradual dissipation, learning its nuances. Whatever stirs beyond the mountains, whatever generated those unnerving blue-white flashes, has indeed touched your world. And you, Fenouille Moonwind, will meet it prepared.
