# turn-161 — DM

The tribe, you've come to understand, numbers around fifty souls, a tightly knit group forged by the harsh realities of their arctic home. Among them, roughly fifteen are children, ranging from Lyrawyn's tender age to those on the cusp of adulthood, ready to join the hunters or gatherers. These are the hearts and minds you seek to cultivate, the future of your adopted people. You approach a young mother named Lena, whose observant gaze you'd noticed often lingering during your lessons. She is quiet but possessed of a sharp mind and a gentle demeanor that the children instinctively trust. You explain, through gestures and simple words, the value of shared knowledge, the importance of these new ways of thinking for the tribe's prosperity. Lena listens intently, her eyes flickering between your earnest face and her own child, who is among your pupils. A slow nod, a hesitant smile, and she agrees to join you. With Lena by your side, the lessons become more structured, her voice adding a calming counterpoint to your more expansive explanations, and the children blossom under the combined attention, their understanding deepening with each passing day.
