# turn-230 — PLAYER

I do not move closer when he speaks. Hope makes people speak quickly. Truth does not. "Slowly," I tell him. "You are not dying in this moment." I watch his breathing as he answers, not just his words. --- Before he speaks further, I gesture slightly to the satchel he clutches. "Open it. Slowly. Place what you carry on the ground." I do not touch it. I study it from where I stand: any markings, stitching, symbols, or script-- I let their meaning come to me without assumption. --- I turn slightly, just enough to speak without breaking focus. "Water," I say to the healer. But I add clearly: "Do not touch him. Leave it within reach." If he has strength to drink, he will. If he does not, that tells me more. --- Then I look back at him. "You walked alone," I say. "The land told me that." A pause. "No one followed you. No one hunted you." Another pause. "So I ask again--carefully this time--" "What did you leave behind that did this to you?" --- As he answers, I observe: - does he cough when he speaks, or only when still? - do the red marks spread or remain fixed? - does his voice weaken--or sharpen with urgency? --- If his condition worsens even slightly while speaking, I kneel--not closer to him, but closer to the ground between us. I place my hand against the thawing earth. I reach outward again, quietly: to the smallest creatures nearby. "Have others fallen like this?" "Do bodies remain where he came from?" "Does the sickness move--or stay?" --- Only after I have his answer, and the land's answer, do I decide: whether this is something we treat, contain, or learn from at a distance.
