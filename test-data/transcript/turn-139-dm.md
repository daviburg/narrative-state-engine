# turn-139 — DM

The final weeks before the birth are filled with a potent mix of anticipation and a quiet sense of readiness. You spend moments whispering to your growing belly, sharing the names you've chosen. For a boy, 'Faelan' - blending the elven beauty of "Fae" with a hint of the sturdy, wild essence of Kael's people. For a girl, 'Lyrawyn' - suggesting the lyrical grace of your moon-elf heritage with the strength of the wind-swept plains. These names, like the child they await, are a bridge between two worlds. One cold morning, as the first grey light filtered through the smoke hole of the longhouse, a sharp, undeniable pang seizes you. It is not the familiar flutter, but a deep, rhythmic tightening, a primal summons that leaves no doubt. The midwife, with her knowing eyes, is already by your side, her touch firm and reassuring. Kael's strong hand grasps yours, his gaze a mixture of fear and profound hope. The world outside the birthing alcove, with its ambitious plans for sedentarization and abundance, recedes. There is only this moment, this powerful, ancient act of bringing new life into the harsh, beautiful world you have embraced.
