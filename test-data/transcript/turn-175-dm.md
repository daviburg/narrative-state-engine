# turn-175 — DM

As your belly swells with new life, your thoughts turn to deeper connections with the land and its bounty. A fertilization ritual for the next season takes root in your mind - a blend of the tribe's ancestral reverence for the earth and the arcane energies you command. You envision a ceremony under the pale arctic sky, channeling your pact's subtle power into the frozen soil, whispering ancient incantations to stir the slumbering life beneath. Perhaps the Shaman would lend his own primal magic, harmonizing with yours to bless the seeds for a more abundant harvest. You then survey the longhouse's contents with a craftsman's eye. Wood carving is already a tribal skill, evidenced by the functional tool handles, sturdy spear shafts, and simple, often symbolically adorned bowls. You see potential to introduce finer techniques, to transform mere utility into art. Pottery is more rudimentary; you've seen crude, sun-dried clay vessels, reinforced with sinew or bark, used for storage, but the art of firing clay into durable, waterproof vessels is unknown. This is another area where your knowledge could transform daily life. As for metals, they are almost entirely absent. The tribe relies on bone, stone, wood, and hide. Any metal they possess is likely a rare, meteoric shard, revered and worked into ceremonial pieces, but certainly not available for general crafting. Their isolated existence in this harsh land has shaped their reliance on natural materials.
