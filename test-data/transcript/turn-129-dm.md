# turn-129 — DM

The progress of the communal longhouse fills you with a deep satisfaction, spurring you to work harder. With the stone perimeter firmly in place, the tribe, now fully invested, begins to lay a split log floor. Strong, resilient timbers, selected with your keen eye and understanding of wood's inherent strengths, are brought from the nearby forest. You subtly lend your arcane touch, making them easier to split and fit, ensuring a tight, insulating surface above the cold earth. Next, the framework for the post-and-beam longhouse begins to rise. Kael, with his inherent knowledge of structure and strength, leads the effort of raising the massive uprights. You work alongside him, your nature skills guiding the selection of the straightest, most durable young trees, able to withstand the brutal arctic winds. As the key joints are notched and fitted, the shaman approaches. He doesn't cast a known spell like *Warp Wood* or *Mending*, but instead, he murmurs ancient chants, tracing intricate symbols on the connecting timbers with a mineral-stained finger. A faint, earthy glow emanates from his touch, and the wood seems to settle, gaining an almost supernatural resilience, imbued with the protective spirits of the forest. In the weeks that have passed since your discovery, your belly has begun to swell, a noticeable roundness beneath your furs. The initial tenderness has settled into a constant, gentle presence, and the rhythmic flutter within you has become a familiar, comforting secret. Your movements are still agile, but a new, protective grace has begun to temper your stride, a silent testament to the life you carry.
