# turn-133 — DM

With the longhouse structure complete, a palpable sense of triumph settles over the tribe. Now, attention turns to making the interior a true home. Kael and a group of skilled hands set to work on the central fire pit. They meticulously line a deep hollow with smooth, flat stones, carefully mortared with a specialized clay, creating a safe, insulated hearth that will radiate warmth throughout the structure. Above it, a carefully constructed smoke hole is fashioned, designed to draw the biting smoke upwards and out, leaving the air within clear and breathable. You approach the elder women, and through gestures, you inquire about the tribe's birthing traditions. One older woman, her face a roadmap of experience, steps forward, her eyes gentle and knowing. She is clearly the midwife. You convey your need for a private, sheltered birthing place within the longhouse, along with reserves of fresh, clean water and soft, clean rags. She nods, understanding the urgency in your gaze, and begins to organize the collection of these vital supplies. As the finishing touches are applied, you seek out the shaman once more. He has watched the construction with quiet approval. You gesture towards the completed longhouse, then to your growing belly, conveying the need for protection and blessing for the new life it will shelter. He smiles, a deep, ancient knowing in his eyes. He traces symbols in the air, then approaches the main entrance. With a piece of charcoal, he draws an intricate protective rune above the doorway, muttering a low chant, invoking the spirits of the earth and sky to safeguard all who dwell within. Your belly, now significantly rounded, presses against your furs, a constant, tangible reminder of the precious life that will soon call this longhouse home.
