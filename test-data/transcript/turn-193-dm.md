# turn-193 — DM

At dawn, you kneel at the eastern boundary, the biting wind whipping strands of hair across your face. You place your hand upon the snow, a touch like ice against ice, where the lingering arcane residue whispers with an almost imperceptible chill. You close your eyes, focusing your will, and feed a careful thread of the silent star's power into the fading imprint--not to command it, but to ask it a question, to demand a response from the unseen. The reaction is immediate and overwhelming. A burst of icy clarity floods your senses, momentarily stealing your breath. You don't just feel it; you *see* it, a brief, stark vision. It's not a natural magic, nor one born of flesh and blood. You perceive a towering structure, not of stone or wood, but of dark, geometrically impossible angles that gleam with an internal, blue-white light--the very same unnerving flashes the hunters described. It feels ancient, impossibly old, yet utterly alien, as if carved from the void itself. There's a profound sense of purpose emanating from it, a cold, calculating will that has no kinship with life as you know it. The trail you've been sensing is a byproduct, an exhaust, of this colossal, inert entity, stretching like a dead limb towards the mountains. The vision recedes as quickly as it came, leaving you gasping, your hand numb in the snow, but your mind ablaze with certainty. The world *does* watch back, Fenouille, and what watches is far more vast and terrifying than mere raiders. The time for blind exploration is past. This is not a simple discovery, but a confrontation with the unknown, demanding careful, informed approach. You gather yourself, your eyes now fixed on the distant, jagged peaks where that chilling structure lies hidden.
