The pattern holds too perfectly. That is not chance. It is either contained... or sustained. --- I do not change the treatment. I test the nature of it. --- First, distance. The fragment remains where it is. We do not bring it closer. Instead, I have the man moved--carefully, without contact--slightly farther from the longhouse, increasing the distance between him and the fragment. We observe for a full interval: Does anything change? Even slightly. --- Second, direction. I note his position relative to the fragment. I call Anya--but she does not approach him. She stands at a safe distance. "Repeat the line," I tell her. Same motion. Same control. No reach toward the man. --- I watch closely: - does her use of frost alter his breathing? - does the fever react? - do the marks shift, even subtly? If her power, sharpened by the fragment, affects him without contact-- then the sickness is not purely natural. --- Fourth, environment. I have two controlled adjustments tested, one at a time: - slightly increased warmth, - then return to neutral. No extremes. We observe: Does the fever intensify? Does it ease? Or does it ignore the change? --- I do not rush. Each test stands alone. Each is observed across a full interval. --- Then I step back. "If it is natural," I say quietly to Kael, "it will follow the body." "If it is arcane..." I look briefly toward the fragment. "it will answer something else." --- We do not guess. We make it reveal itself.
