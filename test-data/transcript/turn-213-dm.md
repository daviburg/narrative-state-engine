# turn-213 — DM

Autumn does not linger, and three weeks of steady labor have deepened the chill in the air and sharpened the light. Within the longhouse, the council gathers, their faces etched with the realities of the season and the weight of your words. You do not ask for plans, but for assessment, for what has truly changed. First, the covered growing patch. You walk among the small frames, your keen eyes scanning. The first hard frost claimed much, but not all. Beneath one hide-and-bone frame, a few hardy stalks of arctic dock cling to life, their leaves slightly bruised but undeniably enduring. It is a small victory, but a potent one. "These," you declare, marking them for further propagation, "will show us the way." Borin, beside you, reports on the smaller kiln: some minor cracking on the initial experimental pieces, but the design held, and more importantly, the process proved viable for regular use. The gift-bearing hunters return from the south, their faces relieved, their news cautious. The southern elders accepted your gifts, their initial suspicion softening into wary acceptance. They did not demand anything in return, but their warnings were clear: the caribou herds are indeed leaner, pushed by deeper pressures from the far south--a growing population, a harsher stretch of land. Diplomacy was accepted, but true friendship remains a distant prospect. Your questions about the trapper yield little new. Scouts found no strange glowing stones in the riverbeds, and no other outsiders have passed through. The winter sickness the trapper spoke of remains a distant rumor, an unseen threat yet to cross the vast, desolate stretches. Three weeks of observation have etched the fragment's rhythm into your memory. Its faint pulse aligns almost perfectly with the distant thrum of the mountains, a testament to a shared, underlying cycle. The boundary, you confirm, has remained stable since your return, neither advancing nor receding further, suggesting a controlled, predictable influence. Anya approaches, clutching a series of crude, evocative drawings. Her dreams, she confirms, intensified dramatically after your departure, filled with geometric shapes and cold, silent presences. You take a shard of ice, not much bigger than your thumb, and lay it on a stone. "Can you alter the frost along this line?" you ask gently, pointing with your finger. Anya closes her eyes, concentrating. A fine, almost imperceptible line of rime forms along the edge of the ice, a nascent, delicate power, measurable but clearly not yet mastered. Kael, his face grim but steady, delivers the hunters' report. Hunting returns have seen a slight improvement under the new patterns, a testament to efficiency, but the overall scarcity of game due to the southern tribes' pressure is undeniable. No sickness has shown among your people, a blessing you attribute to your new provisions and vigilance. "We have worked long enough," you say, your voice carrying through the longhouse. "The world has had time to answer." The answers are complex, Fenouille, weaving together opportunity and threat, discovery and uncertainty. The winter approaches, a crucible for your tribe's newfound strength.
