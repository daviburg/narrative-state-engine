# turn-041 — DM

You continue to work alongside the women, picking up a sprig of moonpetal and holding it up, making an inquisitive sound and gesturing as if asking for its name. The woman next to you, who had previously given a small nod of approval, offers a faint, hesitant smile. She makes a soft, almost hushing sound and shakes her head gently, then points to the herb and makes a series of soft clicks and guttural murmurs that sound nothing like a word. It's clear she's trying to communicate, but the sounds are alien to your ears, and she seems unable to simplify them into something more teachable. Despite your efforts, a formal lesson in their tongue doesn't materialize. The women simply continue their work, occasionally glancing at you with increased curiosity, but offering no further attempts at verbal instruction. The shared silence as you sort and prepare the herbs, however, is no longer entirely awkward. A sense of shared purpose fills the space, slowly eroding the initial apprehension that had hung heavy in the cold night air.
