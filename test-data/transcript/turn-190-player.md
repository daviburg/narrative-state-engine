# turn-190 — PLAYER

I listen carefully to what the wild has revealed and gather the hunters beneath the morning sky. The herds move early, driven by pressure we do not yet see. From this day forward, our hunts change. No party travels blind. Two walk ahead as pathfinders, reading sign and speaking with the land before arrows are loosed. One remains always near the longhouse as watcher. We hunt with patience, not hunger. To the tribe I speak calmly: the world shifts, as it always has in spring. We prepare not for war, but for change. Firewood is gathered. Watches strengthened. The eastern paths observed but not yet walked. Later, I invite Anya to walk with me beyond the noise of the longhouse. I ask her of dreams, of feelings she cannot name, and I teach her first to listen--to wind, to animals, to the quiet spaces between thoughts. Power comes later. Understanding must come first. Each dawn I walk the eastern boundary, tracing the fading arcane trail, learning its rhythm before choosing whether we follow it. Whatever stirs beyond the mountains has already touched our world. We will meet it prepared, not surprised
