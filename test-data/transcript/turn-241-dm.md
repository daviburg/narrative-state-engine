# turn-241 — DM

You shift your perception, letting the world's raw, physical form recede, replaced by the shimmering tapestry of arcane energies. You open your arcane sight fully, not toward the distant hum of the fragment, but directly into the man before you. He is no longer just flesh and failing organs; he is a complex, terrifying pattern. The fever, a burning tide in his blood, is not chaotic; it moves with an unnerving, almost mechanical rhythm, a series of precise, oscillating pulses that seem to feed upon his very life force. The red marks on his skin are not random eruptions, but intricate, geometric lesions, following pathways that seem to mirror the flow of his veins, yet are subtly alien. They pulse with a faint, sickly green luminescence beneath the surface, a cold fire that seems to consume from within. His ragged breath, too, carries a hidden cadence, each gasp part of a larger, deliberate sequence. Something within him repeats with chilling intent, a structured contagion that uses his body as a canvas for its grim design. You compare it to the fragment, to Anya's controlled frost. The patterns are different, yet share a fundamental, unsettling precision. This is not the wild magic of nature, nor the raw power of your pact; it is something crafted, something programmed. What is within him does not belong to the body that carries it; it is a sterile, invasive intelligence. "Tell me," you say, your voice hushed, your gaze fixed on the man's face, "When the marks appeared... did they come all at once... Or did they follow a path?" He struggles for breath, a fresh spasm of coughing wracking his frame. "A path," he rasps, his eyes wide with a desperate memory. "Always a path. First a line, small, like a scratch... on the arm... then another... to the chest... then others... spreading out like roots..." His voice trails off, succumbing to another fit of racking coughs.
