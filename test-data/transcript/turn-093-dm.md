# turn-093 — DM

He accepts the cup, his fingers brushing yours as he takes it, and a flicker of something unreadable passes through his dark eyes before he turns his attention back to his kill. You stay by his side, silently helping him with the heavy carcass, pulling on a leg to help him maneuver it towards the butchering area, your presence a quiet comfort in the organized chaos. He glances at you occasionally, a small, almost imperceptible nod of thanks acknowledging your assistance. As the fires are stoked higher and the rich scent of roasting meat begins to fill the air, the camp settles into a celebratory mood. Laughter rings out, and stories of the hunt are exchanged in low, rumbling tones. You observe the villagers, their movements becoming more relaxed, their expressions softening in the flickering firelight. While the atmosphere is warm and communal, it's a grounded, pragmatic joy. There are no instruments, no spontaneous songs, and no overt displays of performance. The tribe's celebration seems to be one of shared sustenance and survival, their focus on the bounty and the warmth of the hearth rather than elaborate entertainment. The opportunity for a performance dance doesn't seem to present itself naturally within their current customs.
