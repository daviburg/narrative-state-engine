# turn-288 — DM

"We have acted," you state quietly, standing at the center of the longhouse. The hum of the southern shamans' learning, the clatter of the crafters, the soft cries of infants--all fall silent as you gather your council: Kael, the Shaman, Lena, the healer, and the now integrated southern elders and their shamans. "Now we understand what has changed." Kael inclines his head. "While we walked east, the land did not wait. The herds move differently now, Fenouille. The fields have pushed them, creating new routes, denser concentrations in some valleys. Our hunts are more predictable, less strenuous. They return not as before, but with a new awareness of territory, as if the land itself has shifted its breathing." Lena and the healer speak of the tribe. "Strength is up, Fenouille," Lena reports, her eyes bright. "The children, especially, are thriving. They mimic Anya's movements, drawing patterns in the snow. They are learning discipline, not just rote. What has grown without asking, perhaps, is a deeper sense of purpose." The healer adds, "The fevers of winter were minimal. The knowledge of care, the protocols... they are already instinct." Your gaze softens as it turns inward, a hand resting lightly against your side, where Lyrawyn's nascent power and Faelan's quiet strength grow alongside the new life. "Life does not wait for certainty," you muse. "What do the children see now that they did not before? They see order in the storm, a path through the unknown." The Shaman steps forward, his ancient eyes alight. "The fragment, Fenouille, it no longer fights. It listens. Its dreams, if it has them, are muted. The silence between things... it is deeper now, more respectful. Something listens, yes. Something new." You turn to the southern elders. "You came with discipline. You learned with care. And you held your boundary as we held ours." You gesture to the shared work. "The sickness does not belong to one tribe. Nor does the knowledge that stops it. We will continue to teach. Those who can repeat the method without error will carry it. Those who cannot... will remain and learn until they can." "The land is changing," you continue, the truth settling like gravity. "The herds move differently. The sickness travels. And now... the pattern itself can be shaped." You walk outside, Kael joining you. "We do not prepare for winter anymore. We prepare for what comes after it." "We will need more than survival," you tell him. "Food that lasts beyond season. Paths that remain open. Structures that do not depend on chance." To Lena: "The children will not only learn what is. They will learn what can be built." To the Shaman: "We will test where the land yields... and where it resists." You look across the settlement, then beyond it, seeing not just what it is, but what it could become. "We shaped one force. Now we begin shaping everything else." You wait for the world, and the people within it, to answer what is ready to grow.
