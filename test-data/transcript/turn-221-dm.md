# turn-221 — DM

The frost settles, impossibly sharp, holding the symbol in stark relief upon the stone. You do not speak immediately, letting the quiet hum of the fragment fill the space. Power revealed too quickly, you know, is often misunderstood. You step forward, placing your hand near--but not on--the fragment, feeling the cold, precise resonance it radiates. "Again," you say quietly to Anya, your voice a calm counterpoint to the heightened tension. "But slower. No more strength than before. Only control." You watch her closely this time. Her small hand moves with more deliberate grace. The frost forms at a slower, steadier pace, yet the edges remain just as sharp, the pattern as perfectly rendered. The fragment's pulse holds steady, an unchanging hum between attempts. The amplification, you realize, is not a surge of energy, but a consistent property, a chilling lens through which nascent power is focused. You turn to the Shaman, his ancient eyes fixed on the fragment. "This is not chance," you state. "We begin a practice." From this night forward, rules are established: the fragment is never used alone, every use meticulously recorded, every pattern drawn first, then traced, no increase in scale without repeated mastery at smaller sizes. Anya, you clarify, does not train power; she trains precision under influence. A boundary is marked around the stone, a stark line that no one is to cross without purpose. "Children observe only when guided," you instruct. "This is not a fire to gather around." It is something colder, something demanding respect and distance. For a second test, you draw two identical symbols--one near the fragment, the other several paces away. "Trace both," you tell Anya, "Same motion. Same intent." This is not about magic, but about measuring *difference*. You look to Kael, then to the Shaman, drawing them into the profound implications. "The mountain does not speak," you say, "But it has begun to teach." Then, almost as an afterthought, a thought that sends a shiver down your spine: "If this place changes how power behaves... others will feel it." You do not look toward the mountains, but at what you have brought back from them, a chilling, potent new aspect of the world now nestled at the heart of your tribe.
