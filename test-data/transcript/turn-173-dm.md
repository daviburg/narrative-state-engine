# turn-173 — DM

The following months pass in a rhythm of quiet growth and diligent work. The cleared perimeters around the longhouse now allow for an unobstructed view, and the strategically placed sound traps, though yet untested by intruders, offer a subtle comfort. Tala and Elara continue their work, their practiced hands ensuring a steady supply of preserved foods, while Lena's voice blends with yours in the longhouse, guiding the children through their lessons. The tribe, once a purely nomadic band, begins to fully embrace the stability and abundance you've introduced, the longhouse becoming a true hearth of their evolving culture. Your attention, however, is often drawn to the small, cultivated patches near the longhouse. You observe the tiny, resilient sprouts, monitoring their progress with a fierce, protective maternal instinct. The first tentative harvest of arctic dock and fireweed shoots, though modest, is a triumph--a tangible symbol of your foresight and the tribe's adaptability. Each day, your belly rounds further, a living clock measuring the passage of time, the subtle shifts and movements within a constant reminder of the new life soon to join your growing family. The community watches, sharing in your anticipation, as the season progresses, holding its breath for the arrival of this second child.
