All paths have answered. Now we decide. --- I gather Kael, the Shaman, the healer, Anya, and the southern shamans. "This is not one problem," I say. "It is three." "And we do not solve them in sequence." "We solve them together." --- First: the sickness. I look to the southern shamans. "You will learn the method." "But not all at once." "You will observe first." "You will see it done again." "Then you will repeat it under our watch." "No deviation." "If you carry this knowledge incorrectly, you will spread death, not stop it." --- I designate one controlled case. Not many. One. "If you can save one," I tell them, "then you can be trusted to save more." --- Second: the boundary. I turn to Kael. "The longhouse remains closed." "No one enters." "No contact without protocol." "But we do not turn them away." "We meet them here--between worlds." This becomes a place of teaching. Not of merging. --- Third: the source. Now I turn east. "The sickness has a beginning." "And anything with a pattern has a maker--or a place it was made possible." --- I choose a small expedition. Not many. Only those who can move, observe, and return. - myself, - one hunter (Rurik), - one who can read the land, - and one who can survive without being seen. Not Anya. Not yet. Her place is here, ensuring the method holds. --- "We follow the trapper's path." "We study the stones." "We do not confront what we do not understand." "We return with knowledge--or not at all." --- To the man--the survivor--I say: "You will draw for us." "Every path you remember." "Every place the sickness began." "You will help us walk it without stepping where you fell." --- To the Shaman, quietly: "If the fragment has changed..." "then what lies east is already awake." --- Then I step back. Not to act immediately. But to let each part begin: - the teaching, - the boundary, - the preparation. --- "We do not wait for the world to choose for us," I say. "We choose how we meet it." --- Before we depart, I do not rush the expedition. "We prepare," I say simply. --- I review what I carry--not as a warrior, but as one who must return. "What we lack will decide what kills us." --- To Borin and the crafters: "I need three things--not for strength, but for certainty." - A reinforced head covering--fur, leather, bone--something that turns aside cold, fall, or chance. - A second-hand tool--not a weapon, but utility: something that can block, carry, or interrupt. - A ranged option--simple, reliable, quiet. I do not chase what I cannot reach. --- To the traders and the southern delegation: "We exchange knowledge for tools." Not gifts--trade. I seek: - small objects worn on the body--charms, carved bone, worked stone--anything believed to ward, steady, or protect - items their shamans trust, not for power, but for consistency "If your people survive, something you carry works," I tell them. "I want to know what." --- To the Shaman: "Nothing loud. Nothing that draws attention." "If something can anchor the mind, resist influence, or reveal hidden pattern--I take it." --- To the healer: "What do I carry if I fall ill away from you?" I assemble: - controlled rations - water discipline tools - known safe roots only - nothing untested --- To myself, quietly: "I do not leave gaps." --- Nothing ornate. Nothing excessive. Everything chosen for a reason. Everything something I can explain. --- When I stand ready again, I do not look stronger. I look... complete. --- Then, only then: "We move at first light."
