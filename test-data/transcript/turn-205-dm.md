# turn-205 — DM

The mountain has answered once, and for tonight, that is enough. You carefully wrap the dark fragment in layered cloth and place it precisely at the center of your camp, equidistant from Rurik and Boruk's bedrolls and your own. Around it, you mark a small circle in the frost, scoring reference lines that point directly toward the distant, thrumming peaks. Before settling, you concentrate, comparing the fragment's faint, rhythmic pulse to the slow, deep beat emanating from the mountains, committing the subtle synchronization to memory. "We learn nothing more by pressing tonight," you tell your companions quietly. "Now we learn whether the mountain changes the world when unwatched." The fire is doused early, plunging the camp into near-total darkness, illuminated only by the cold, distant stars. You and the hunters take alternating watches, your senses heightened, observing without interference. The fragment's pulse remains a steady, almost imperceptible hum, neither strengthening nor weakening. Frost continues its slow creep across the landscape, unperturbed by its presence. No sudden gusts of wind, no unsettling dreams, no inexplicable sounds disturb the watchful night. The world, outwardly, holds its breath. At dawn, before breaking camp, you immediately return to the snow-carved boundary markings. The sterile line, precise and clear from the previous evening, has *receded* slightly, drawing back from its previous maximum reach by no more than a hand's breadth. It is an almost imperceptible withdrawal, yet it is a change, a confirmation that the construct's influence is dynamic, not static, and that its response to your query was a measured, transient burst. The sterile presence is not actively pushing outward, but maintaining a subtle, controlled domain. You commit this vital piece of information to memory. The mountain may be powerful, but it is not lunging. The time for caution has given way to carefully considered advance.
