You choose a second location along the boundary, a separate edge of the system's influence, far enough from the first to be treated as an independent challenge. Here, the hexagonal melt patterns still assert themselves, the crystalline growths shimmer with their sterile light. "We repeat it," you instruct Anya, "The same spacing. The same form. No adjustment." With precise, practiced movements, she lays down the three minimal disturbances that define the second triangular region. The Shaman's Echo-Song continues, a consistent, low resonance, now encompassing a broader, if subtly more strained, field. You step back, your arcane sight now encompassing both regions of disruption. The second triangle, once formed, produces the same immediate weakening observed in the first. Within its newly defined bounds, the melt patterns fragment, the crystalline growth visibly retracts, and the subtle, structured energy of the pattern struggles to maintain coherence. It behaves, in essence, identically to its predecessor, confirming the repeatability of your method. However, the system's overall strain is palpably increased. The river stones, the heart of this localized phenomenon, now pulse with profound instability. Their blue light flickers, not just stuttering occasionally, but dimming to a faint, erratic glow for extended periods. The rhythmic desynchronization observed earlier intensifies into a jarring, chaotic thrum, as if the very mechanism driving the pattern is struggling to maintain its basic function. There is a noticeable, sustained drop in their intensity, and the metallic shimmer in the water around them becomes more pronounced, hinting at an expenditure of energy to counter the widespread disarray. The system does not treat both triangles independently. The strain from one clearly amplifies the effect of the other, pushing the overall pattern closer to true collapse. It is as if the continuous, divided effort to maintain perfection at two distinct points is simply too much. You hold for a full interval, allowing the profound changes to resonate through the landscape. "If this can be repeated," you say quietly, a new certainty in your voice, "then it is no longer discovery. It is method." The system, once seemingly unyielding, is now demonstrably vulnerable to a widespread, methodical application of imperfection.
