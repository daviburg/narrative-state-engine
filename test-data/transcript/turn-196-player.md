# turn-196 — PLAYER

The mountains rise sharper with each mile, their jagged edges cutting against the pale sky. The air grows thinner, and the silence deepens--not the natural quiet of snow, but a stillness that feels watched. On the third evening, when we make camp within sight of the highest ridge, I do not simply observe the alien presence from afar. I step beyond the firelight and draw a slow circle in the frost with the butt of my staff. Within it, I place a shard of clear ice and a fragment of obsidian taken from our hearthstone. I call upon the silent star--not to shield me, not to conceal me--but to announce me. "We have followed your trail," I murmur into the dark. "We come not as prey nor conqueror. We seek to understand what stirs in these mountains." Then I open my senses fully and allow the sterile resonance to find me, if it will. Rurik and Boruk stand ready, but I do not draw steel. If the construct watches, let it know we approach awake.
