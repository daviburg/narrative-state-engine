# turn-326 — DM

You take Kael with you, walking alone just beyond the immediate perimeter of the settlement, the crisp air of early winter biting at your cheeks. You recount the adventures, the years, the births of Lyrawyn and Faelan, and the growing life within you. You guide his strong, calloused hand to your belly, letting him feel the gentle stir of the fourth child. "Kael, your strength, your story lives strong in our children. We made them well, and we made them each so unique. You have seen around our tribe how I nurtured diversity into resilience and growth." His hand rests, warm and firm, over the life within you, a quiet pride blooming in his eyes as he feels the subtle movement. He nods slowly, his gaze fixed on the distant, silent world. "I have seen it, Fenouille," he rumbles, his voice deep. "Your wisdom... it shapes more than just the land." You continue, "You also learn how uniquely different I am, how I plan far ahead for your tribe, our tribe, how I always have the best in mind for it, like I know you do. You have four, your legacy is secured, your bloodline will keep the tribe strong for generations to come. Gorok... Gorok is formidable. He brings strength to our settlement only he possesses. Yet, he is alone and his strength risks dying with him. If he doesn't find a suitable mate to bear him children by the time your fourth is born, what do you think I should do to ensure his bloodline is extended, and I bring even more diversity into mine?" Kael's hand tenses slightly on your belly, his breath hitching for a moment. He turns to you, his dark eyes searching yours, a flicker of something raw and possessive battling with the deep, unwavering trust he holds for your vision. He looks back at the distant outline of the settlement, then at the empty expanse of snow, his brow furrowed in thought. The raw, primal instinct of his nature struggles with the strategic, patterned logic you present. "Gorok's strength is a mountain," he says at last, his voice a low, gravelly rasp. "A mountain needs a base. A root." He pauses, weighing his words, choosing them with the same care he would a path through treacherous snow. "Your mind, Fenouille, sees beyond what mine can grasp. You seek strength, always. I... I trust your purpose, even when the path is... unexpected." He takes his hand from your belly, reaching instead to cup your cheek, his thumb gently stroking your skin. "Before such a... profound step," he continues, his voice softer, "perhaps we seek a suitable root for *him*. A woman, from the migrating tribes, or even from the Riverfolk. A woman who matches his fire, his purpose. One who understands his strength, and desires to bind it, and bear it forth." He looks at you, his gaze intense, a deep, silent plea for a solution that honors both the tribe's need and the bonds you share. "If such a one cannot be found, Fenouille... then your wisdom, as always, will show the truest way. Your children... they are the strength of this place. And your mind... it ensures they endure."
