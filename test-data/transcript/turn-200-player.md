# turn-200 — PLAYER

The pulse travels through the mountains like the beat of some frozen heart, but I do not move toward it. Ancient engines do not reveal their core to strangers -- they reveal their excess. I lower my hand from the obsidian shard and instead turn my attention to the land between us and the peaks. If the construct has answered, then something in this range must now carry its echo. I instruct Rurik and Boruk to remain behind me and begin searching for signs not of the structure itself, but of its influence: Stones warmed or chilled unnaturally. Snow melted in geometric patterns. Wildlife avoiding a precise boundary. Crystalline growths or metallic residue in the frost. Fallen fragments carried by the pulse. If this entity radiates power, it must leave artifacts at its margins. We do not climb the mountain tonight. We harvest its shadow. If this mountain heart beats, it must have arteries. We will map the veins, not strike the heart.
