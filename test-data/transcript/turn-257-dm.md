# turn-257 — DM

"All paths have answered," you declare, gathering Kael, the Shaman, the healer, Anya, and the assembled southern shamans. Your gaze is unwavering, encompassing each face. "This is not one problem. It is three. And we do not solve them in sequence. We solve them together." To the southern shamans, you lay down the terms of learning. "You will observe first. You will see it done again. Then you will repeat it under our watch. No deviation. If you carry this knowledge incorrectly, you will spread death, not stop it." A single controlled case is designated for their instruction. "If you can save one, then you can be trusted to save more." Kael receives renewed instruction regarding the boundary. "The longhouse remains closed. No one enters. No contact without protocol. But we do not turn them away. We meet them here--between worlds." This cleared space becomes a place of teaching, a crucible where knowledge is forged, but not mingled carelessly. Then, your gaze turns east. "The sickness has a beginning. And anything with a pattern has a maker--or a place it was made possible." A small expedition is chosen: yourself, the seasoned hunter Rurik, a silent tracker who can read the land, and an unseen presence, a scout capable of moving through the wilderness without drawing attention. Anya remains behind, her role now anchoring the method you've established. "We follow the trapper's path. We study the stones. We do not confront what we do not understand. We return with knowledge--or not at all." The man, your first survivor, is tasked with drawing. "Every path you remember. Every place the sickness began. You will help us walk it without stepping where you fell." To the Shaman, you speak softly, a chilling observation. "If the fragment has changed, then what lies east is already awake." You step back, not for immediate action, but to set each part in motion: the intensive teaching begins, the boundary around the southern delegation solidifies, and the preparations for your journey commence. "We do not wait for the world to choose for us," you assert. "We choose how we meet it." You review what you carry, not as a warrior, but as one who must return. "What we lack will decide what kills us." To Borin and the crafters, your instructions are precise: "Three things--not for strength, but for certainty. A reinforced head covering, a second-hand tool, and a simple, reliable ranged option." To the southern delegation, you propose a trade: "We exchange knowledge for tools." You seek small, personal objects of warding, items their shamans trust for their consistent protective properties. "If your people survive, something you carry works," you explain. "I want to know what." From the Shaman, you request quiet things: "Nothing loud. Nothing that draws attention. If something can anchor the mind, resist influence, or reveal hidden pattern--I take it." The healer is instructed on what provisions you'll need if illness strikes far from the longhouse: controlled rations, water purification tools, and only known, safe roots. No untested remedies. "I do not leave gaps," you think, your preparations meticulous, devoid of ornament, everything chosen for a reason. When you stand ready again, you do not look stronger, but complete. "We move at first light."
