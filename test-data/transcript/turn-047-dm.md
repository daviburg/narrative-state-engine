# turn-047 — DM

As the meal concludes, the fire begins to die down, casting longer, dancing shadows across the camp. The villagers start to disperse, their movements quiet and efficient. You notice several crude lean-tos and simple tents, woven from hide and branches, dotting the perimeter beyond the immediate firelight. Into these, families retreat, children carried or guided by their parents. There are indeed young ones, wrapped snugly in furs, their sleepy faces occasionally illuminated by the fading embers. The women, after ensuring the meal remnants are tidied, tend to their children or settle into their own designated shelters. There's no distinct ritual or activity that singles them out for any particular 'night duty' beyond their family roles. A few figures, presumably the watchmen you observed earlier, take up positions at the camp's edges, their forms merging with the deep shadows of the forest. The elder remains by the central fire for a time, a solitary, thoughtful figure, before eventually rising and heading towards a larger, more enclosed structure.
