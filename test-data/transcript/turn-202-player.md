# turn-202 — PLAYER

I do not rush toward the mountain. Power radiates -- and radiance leaves residue. I kneel beside the hexagonal melt pattern first, not touching its center. With the butt of my staff, I trace the boundary, testing whether the geometry is stable or fluctuating. If I disturb its edge, does the pattern resist correction? Does it reform? Or does it collapse? Next, I instruct Rurik to collect one of the dark fragments using leather wrapping, not bare skin. It is placed inside a bone charm pouch, separated from direct contact with flesh. I do not yet claim it as ours. I study it with arcane sight. Does its energy pulse in rhythm with the mountain? Does it diminish with distance? Does it react to my pact energy? Does it respond differently to cold iron or obsidian? Then I turn to the crystalline growths. I break one carefully using the flat of my blade and observe: Does it fracture like stone? Does it emit light or sound when broken? Does the boundary line shift as pieces are removed? If this mountain heart beats, then its arteries can be mapped. Before dawn, I carve subtle markings into the snow to mark the edge of the sterile boundary and compare whether it advances or recedes by morning. We do not conquer. We test. If the fragment remains stable in containment, I will carry one piece no larger than a thumb joint. The rest we bury beneath marked stone, to observe whether removal alters the boundary.
