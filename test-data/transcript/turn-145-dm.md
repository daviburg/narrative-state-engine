# turn-145 — DM

The first few months of Lyrawyn's life are a gentle cocoon within the warmth of the longhouse. The biting arctic winds rage outside, but inside, you are safe, surrounded by the quiet hum of communal life and the fierce, protective love of Kael. Your days are filled with the soft rhythms of motherhood: feeding, comforting, and simply watching your daughter grow, her tiny hands and feet already hinting at the strength she will inherit. Yet, your mind, ever active, seeks purpose beyond the immediate demands of infancy. The tribe, you realize, lives with a primal simplicity, their ingenuity focused on survival. Fermentation, the subtle magic of transforming simple ingredients, is likely unknown to them. You begin to inquire, through gestures and what limited words you now share, about any sources of wild honey. Small amounts, gathered in the warmer months and stored, are eventually brought to you. With meticulous care, you set about creating your first batch of mead. It is a slow, patient process, combining the precious honey with purified snowmelt and allowing the wild yeasts, drawn from the air or perhaps subtly encouraged by a whisper of your own magic, to begin their work. The scent, initially sweet, slowly transforms into something richer, more complex. This new endeavor becomes your quiet project, a testament to your heritage and a potential new gift for your adopted people, while Lyrawyn sleeps soundly beside you, growing stronger with each passing day.
