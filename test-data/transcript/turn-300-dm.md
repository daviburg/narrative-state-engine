# turn-300 — DM

"These are not accidents," you repeat, letting the silence hang heavy in the air, allowing the weight of your words to settle upon the council. "But neither are they yet ours." You draw three smaller marks beneath your previous lines--"Observed. Understood. Used."--a clear hierarchy for the path ahead. "We are still at the first." You turn to the hardened grass. "This is not just growth," you tell the healer and Kael, your voice precise, "it is selection." You instruct them to isolate a small plot, observing if it spreads, replaces, or changes the soil, strictly forbidding its large-scale planting or use as primary food. "Not yet." Lena receives clear guidelines regarding the bird's nest. "The children will not copy this; they will study it." One group is to observe its structure, another its placement. They will then construct similar forms away from the original, meticulously comparing whether the pattern can be repeated as knowledge or if it remains a mere, ephemeral influence. Holding the vibrant petal of the rare bloom, you address the healer. "This is neither plant nor pattern alone. Treat it as potent." You outline a strict protocol of small, measured tests: for strength, for recovery, for reaction to the sick--all to be meticulously recorded, with "no assumptions." To the Shaman and Anya, you explain the profound interconnectedness. "These three are not separate. They are the same question, answered in different forms." Their task becomes precise mapping: where the grass grows, where the nests form, where the bloom appears. "If they overlap, we study that place. If they separate, we learn why." A quieter, more somber instruction follows, meant for all. "We do not spread this. Not to the southern tribes. Not yet. What we do not understand, we do not export." The southern elders listen, their faces a mix of understanding and deference, as you reiterate that they may observe but not carry this knowledge back until it is correctly named. Finally, you step back, your gaze sweeping across the now-attentive faces. "We have shaped one force already. This may be the result. Or the beginning of another." Your hand rests instinctively on your growing belly, a silent acknowledgment of the life within. "We move carefully now. Because this--is how something new begins."
