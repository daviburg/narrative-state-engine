You pick up the small carving tool, its polished bone cool against your fingertips. Moving closer to the group of women, you settle down beside them, watching their practiced movements. As you begin to sort through the dried bundles, a few familiar scents and textures trigger recognition. You identify dried moonpetal, known for its calming properties, and a pungent root that resembles frost-bite balm, a common remedy in colder climes. Using the fine tip of the tool, you carefully scrape away withered leaves from a sprig of what you identify as winter's breath, a rare herb used in soothing teas. The women exchange glances, a hint of surprise in their eyes, and one of them offers a small, approving nod, her expression softening. The shared task, and your unexpected knowledge, begins to bridge the silent chasm between you.
