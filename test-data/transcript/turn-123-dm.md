# turn-123 — DM

Wrapped in the furs, as the first pale light of dawn touched the edges of your shared dwelling, you turned to Kael. His eyes, still heavy with sleep, met yours. You placed his hand gently on your belly, a tender smile on your lips, and then, slowly, deliberately, you traced a circle over the spot where new life stirred. You looked into his eyes, allowing all the hope and wonder of the discovery to shine through your own. A beat passed, then understanding dawned in his gaze, a profound mixture of surprise, awe, and a fierce, protective joy. He brought your hand to his lips, kissing your palm before pressing his forehead against yours. Later, as the camp began to stir, your thoughts turned to the rough, hide-and-snow shelters. They had been sufficient for survival, but for a child, for *your* child, they would need more. You began to envision how your arcane arts could transform these meager dwellings. Stronger hides, woven with enduring enchantments. Foundations reinforced by earth-shaping cantrips. Perhaps even a subtle ward against the biting cold and the unseen spirits of the wild. Your role here was shifting; no longer just an outsider adapting, but a protector, a builder, ready to elevate this tribe, this family, for the new life soon to join them.
