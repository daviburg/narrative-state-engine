# turn-181 — DM

The final days of your pregnancy are spent in a warm haze of anticipation. The longhouse, vibrant with the murmurs of children and the scents of drying herbs and newly fired clay, feels almost too small for your expansive dreams. You envision a future where another longhouse, perhaps even more, stands beside this one, each brimming with the laughter and life of your progeny, and the progeny of others. You voice these hopes to the women of the tribe during communal meals and while working together, painting vivid pictures of a strong, numerous people, unbowed by the arctic's challenges. They listen, some with shy smiles, others with thoughtful expressions. As for the pairings, in this harsh land where every hand contributes to survival, most adult women are indeed paired with a man. Life here encourages such unions, for companionship, for shared labor, and most crucially, for the continuation of the tribe. There might be a few exceptions--an older widow, perhaps, or a younger woman not yet of age to truly seek a mate--but the societal norm is clear: partnerships are essential, and most women find a partner early in their adult lives, solidifying the bonds that hold the tribe together.
