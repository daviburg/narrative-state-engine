# turn-235 — DM

"Fever. Then breath. Then marks. No recovery." You listen to the man's ragged confession without interruption, the grim pattern solidifying in your mind. Patterns, however, were merely observations until tested. You turn to the healer, your voice low and resolute. "We do not try to save him by force. We give his body the chance to fight." Your instructions are precise, leaving no room for misunderstanding. "He is not to be made stronger quickly; he is to be made steady." Warm furs are provided, but not in excess, lest they overwhelm his fevered frame. Small sips of water are offered regularly, never a deluge. Only crushed roots that have been meticulously identified are to be administered--nothing unknown. All interactions are to be tool-mediated; no direct contact. "We observe whether the fever rises or breaks. If the body is fighting, we support it. If it is failing, we learn how." You kneel again, maintaining your distance, your disguised gaze fixed on him. "Your body is burning," you tell the man, your voice calm. "Do not fight that. Tell me--when the fever rose in others... did it ever fall before they died?" The healer is instructed to mark time, for the duration between stages, you emphasize, matters more than the stages themselves. Then, your voice hardens, carrying over to the attentive tribe. "If he dies, nothing remains. No burial. No leaving to the ground. No delay." You look directly at Kael, your decree absolute. "We burn the body completely. Not until it blackens--until it is gone. The fire is watched. The remains are not touched. What is left is scattered where nothing grows and nothing drinks." You gesture to the ground beneath the man, now consecrated by his suffering. "This place is not used again. Snow, ash, and time will take it. No one walks here without purpose." Quieter, you add, "If the earth takes the bodies... we do not give it more." You look at the man, not as a victim, but as a test. "We will see," you say calmly, "whether this ends the same way--or not."
