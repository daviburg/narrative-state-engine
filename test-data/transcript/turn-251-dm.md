# turn-251 — DM

"We stop the disruption," you declare quietly, the new phase of the struggle beginning. "It no longer needs to stumble. It needs to fail." You kneel, observing the fractured pattern within the man. It no longer moves with malevolent intent; it merely hesitates, a chaotic echo of its former precision. This, you decide, is enough. The method shifts. No more cutting the leading edge. Now, the goal is to prevent it from ever becoming whole again. "No lines. No crossing. Only presence," you instruct Anya. With delicate movements, she traces faint, incomplete shapes in the air, a whisper of unseen magic, just enough to disturb without defining, to prevent the pattern from finding its anchors. The Shaman's Echo-Song continues, but softer now, a sustained, gentle hum designed to keep the pattern from remembering its original, deadly shape. To the healer, your instructions change fundamentally: "We strengthen the man." More water, carefully increased. Small but more frequent nourishment. Controlled warmth--enough to support his healing body, not to feed the remnants of the pattern. "The body must take ground." You return to arcane sight, now looking for a different outcome. Does the pattern attempt to re-form with structure, or does it collapse into irregular fragments? What you witness is a slow, agonizing dissolution. The once-precise lines within him fray, becoming increasingly haphazard. The green luminescence flickers erratically, losing its synchronized pulse. It collapses into irregular fragments. You do not interfere further, allowing his body the space to clear what remains. To the man, you offer words of quiet encouragement. "You are past the worst. Now you do less, not more. Let it fade." The days pass, and with each one, the alien pattern within him continues its slow, inevitable collapse. The green luminescence eventually ceases to pulse entirely, becoming dull, dead spots that fade into his healing flesh. The rhythm no longer returns, replaced by the faint, healthy beat of his own struggling heart. What remains is only a frail body, slowly mending from a battle fought on the edge of life and death. You rise slowly, looking out at the thawing landscape. "This is no longer a battle," you say quietly. "It is a clearing. And we do not trample what is already falling." The man is no longer a carrier; the pattern is gone. Now, the true healing, and the understanding of what has been vanquished, can begin.
