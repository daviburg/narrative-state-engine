# turn-085 — DM

You offer the shaman a subtle wink and a warm smile, a silent acknowledgment of the shared, albeit different, currents of power you both sense. He returns your smile, a deeper, less knowing warmth in his eyes this time, and offers a slight, almost imperceptible bow of his head before turning back to his quiet ministrations among the women. Turning your attention back to the camp, you scan the ongoing activities. The rhythmic scraping of furs being cleaned, the low hum of women spinning coarse thread, the crackle of the main fire where a stew pot simmers. You could help mend nets or sort dried provisions, but your thoughts drift to the young hunter, out in the biting cold. An idea sparks. You quietly approach the women tending the hearth and, with gestures and a few simple, repeated words, you inquire about any restorative herbs they might possess that could be steeped into a comforting, warm drink. You offer to prepare a strong, invigorating brew for his return, something to chase the chill from his bones and soothe his weary muscles.
