# turn-308 — DM

You do not rise immediately. Cradling Rune, you remain still, observing not just as a mother, but as one who must understand what has truly entered the world. "What arrived is not only a child," you say quietly, your voice filled with a profound certainty. "It is alignment." You look first to the healer. He approaches, his gaze methodical and calm, examining the infant. "Her body, Fenouille, is perfect. Every part, every function, follows natural form with an exquisite precision. There is no disarray, no aberration, only an almost mathematical harmony in her physical being." The Shaman steps forward, his ancient eyes fixed on the child, then flicking to the distant hum of the fragment. "Her presence," he intones, his voice soft, "does not alter the fragment, but rather resonates with it. It is as if the fragment finds a deeper echo of itself in her, a harmonic, not a disruption." Anya, her young face awestruck, comes closer. "I... I feel her," she whispers, her gaze distant, listening to something only she can perceive. "But it's not like another person, Fenouille. It's like the pattern itself. It's clean. It's... the clearest thing I've ever felt." Lyrawyn, her eyes wide with a mix of wonder and nascent understanding, steps gently forward. "The song," she says, her voice barely audible, "it doesn't change near her, Mama. It becomes easier. Clearer. Like the world finally knows how to sing its own part." You listen to their words, finding the consistency that speaks of truth, not wonder or fear. Then you look to Kael. "No one approaches her without purpose. This is not protection from harm. It is protection from influence." He nods, his face grimly understanding the new, complex boundary. You turn back to the council, your gaze sweeping over them. "We defined balance. Now we test if it holds." You establish three immediate conditions: Rune is never left within a disruption field, never brought near the fragment for extended time, and always observed in both structured and unstructured spaces. "We do not raise her in only one world," you state, "She must exist in both." A quieter, more profound thought follows. "If she stabilizes everything--she becomes a single point of failure. If she disrupts nothing--she may erase what we preserved." You pause, then define her role, not as child, but as function within the greater weave. "She is not the center of the Quiet Weave. She is a thread within it." You look to Lena. "She learns as the others do. No exception. No acceleration. No isolation." Lena's gentle face holds a fierce resolve, acknowledging the immense trust placed in her. Finally, you look down at Rune, a tiny hand grasping your finger. And you listen, not to what she is, but to what changes around her, without anyone acting, a silent symphony of resonance and alignment.
