I do not let momentum disperse. I convert it into commitments. For Thorne, I define exchange. "Your two return," I say, "with what they can already carry without loss." "In their place, you send four--of your choosing. Not the weakest. Not the most desperate. Those who can learn, hold, and decide." Before the trackers depart, I gather them and the two maidens. "This is recorded," I state. A simple ceremony. Witnessed. Named. Not ornament, but commitment. "They leave as more than learners." The maidens speak to them plainly: they will be fathers. "They return before the next summer's birth." I require the outcome: whether they accept the bond, how they speak of it to Thorne, and how Thorne responds when lineage is tied across tribes. For Lyra, I seek intent, not assumption. "You have seen one cycle begin," I tell her. "Winter will show what endures." "You choose." "Remain, and see the Quiet Weave under strain and stillness." "Or return, carry what we built, and come back when the land opens again." I pause, then add the second layer. "If you stay--walk as more than ally." I look to Lyrawyn. "Stand as her second anchor." "Not teacher alone. Witness." I ask it directly. "Will you be named her godmother, and bind your path to hers?" I require the outcome: her decision, and how it shapes her role here and among her own people. For Borin and Faelan, I formalize what has already begun. "Borin," I say, "you are now responsible for him." "Not to shape him into you." "To let what he sees become skill." Faelan remains with him in all safe work. Observation first. Hands when ready. I require the outcome: whether Faelan's instinct becomes repeatable knowledge, or remains singular. For Gorok, I remove all audience. We speak alone. "You built strength here," I say. "Now I ask what you build beyond yourself." Direct. "You have warriors. Do you have heirs?" I do not circle the question. "Do you have a wife? A bond? Or do you leave your strength to end with you?" I let him answer fully. Then I define the possibility without forcing it. "This place does not take what you are." "It extends it." "If you bind yourself here, it is not loss of your tribe." "It is the beginning of something that does not end with one line." I require the outcome: his status, his willingness to bind deeper--through oath, presence, or blood--and the conditions under which he would do so. For the settlement, I finalize winter posture. Faelan with Borin. Rune observed across all spaces. I define one more thread. "The four from Thorne, when they arrive, do not enter fully." "They pass first through boundary, learning, and proof." "No one joins without holding what we built." Then I stop. I do not advance time yet.
