# turn-243 — DM

"This is not fever alone," you state quietly, turning to Kael and the Shaman, your voice firm with the certainty of your arcane sight. "It is a pattern inside him. No one moves closer. The boundary holds." You turn back to the man, his eyes unfocused, but still alight with a faint, desperate hope. "I see it," you tell him, your voice steady. "It moves in lines. It spreads with intent. I will try to interrupt it, not to harm you, but to give your body a chance." You outline your precise instructions: "You must follow exactly. Breathe when I say. Hold when I say. Do not fight the heat. If anything changes--tell me at once." The perimeter remains secured by Kael. The healer stands ready with water, maintaining distance, while the watcher meticulously tracks breath and time. The Shaman positions himself behind you, a silent anchor, his presence steadying the tense air. You kneel between yourself and the man, and with a shard of obsidian, you draw a simple counter-pattern on a flat stone: a straight line bisected by a crossing line--a deliberate break, not a mimicry, of the alien design within him. "Anya," you call, her name cutting through the silence. "Same control. Smaller. Trace only the crossing line. No more. Stop the moment I say." Anya, her gaze fixed on the stone, begins. A whisper of frost, impossibly precise, forms along the crossing line. You watch, arcane sight fully engaged, the pattern within the sick man. As Anya's frost coalesces, the fever's mechanical rhythm within him stutters, a faint, almost imperceptible tremor. The sickly green luminescence of the geometric lesions along his described path seems to flicker, a momentary desynchronization in their internal pulse. His breath, though still ragged, catches, a single, sharp intake before resuming its cadence. "Stop," you command, your voice immediate, cutting the air. Anya's hand stills, the frost pattern hanging, shimmering, incomplete. The effect, though subtle, was undeniable. The pattern had reacted. You turn to the Shaman, your voice quiet now, imbued with a cold certainty. "If this is crafted, it can be disrupted. If it can be disrupted, it can be slowed. And if it can be slowed," you glance to the man, whose breath is now uneven, a new tremor running through him, "it can be beaten." The first attempt has yielded a response. The next decision must be made with utmost care.
