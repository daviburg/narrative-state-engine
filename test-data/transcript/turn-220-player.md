The frost settles, sharper than it has any right to be. I do not speak immediately. Power revealed too quickly is often misunderstood. I step forward and place my hand near--but not on--the fragment, feeling the cold precision it radiates. "Again," I say quietly to Anya. Stabilization "But slower." "No more strength than before. Only control." I watch closely this time: whether the frost forms at the same speed, whether the edges remain as sharp, whether the fragment's pulse shifts between attempts. If the amplification holds steady, then it is not a surge. It is a property. System I turn to the Shaman. "This is not chance." "We begin a practice." From this night forward: the fragment is never used alone, every use is recorded, every pattern drawn first, then traced, no increase in scale without repetition at smaller size. Anya does not train power. She trains precision under influence. Containment I mark a boundary around the stone. "No one approaches this without purpose." "Children observe only when guided." "This is not a fire to gather around." It is something colder. Expansion I prepare a second test. A simple one. I draw two identical symbols. One near the fragment. One beyond its reach. "Trace both," I tell Anya. "Same motion. Same intent." We compare. Not magic. Difference. Meaning I look to Kael, then to the Shaman. "The mountain does not speak." "But it has begun to teach." The Wider World Then I add, almost as an afterthought: "If this place changes how power behaves..." "Others will feel it." Closing I do not look toward the mountains. I look at what we have brought back from them.
