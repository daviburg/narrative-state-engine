# turn-035 — DM

You willingly approach the pile of logs, finding them surprisingly heavy and encrusted with ice. The cold bites at your hands as you hoist them, one by one, carrying them to the roaring bonfire. The rhythmic thud of wood on frozen earth, combined with the crackle and hiss of the flames, fills the quiet night. As you work, you notice the eyes of the villagers upon you. The initial guardedness in their gazes seems to soften, replaced by a quiet observation. The broader figure who first captured you now watches, his spear leaned casually against a nearby tree, his stance less aggressive. You are simply another pair of hands, contributing to the shared warmth and temporary security of the encampment, and the communal effort seems to dissolve some of the earlier tension.
