# turn-250 — PLAYER

I do not order another cut. "We stop the disruption," I say quietly. "It no longer needs to stumble." "It needs to fail." --- I kneel, watching the fractured pattern within him. It no longer moves with intent. It hesitates. That is enough. --- We change the method. No more cutting the leading edge. Now we prevent it from becoming whole again. --- To Anya: "No lines." "No crossing." "Only presence." I have her trace a faint, incomplete shape in the air--not forming, not finishing--just enough to disturb without defining. A whisper, not a blade. --- To the Shaman: "The Echo-Song continues." "But softer now." "I do not want to break it further." "I want to keep it from remembering its shape." --- To the healer: "We strengthen the man." Now we shift: - more water, carefully increased, - small but more frequent nourishment, - controlled warmth--enough to support, not to feed the remnants. "The body must take ground." --- I return to arcane sight. I look for only one thing: Does the pattern attempt to re-form with structure? Or does it collapse into irregular fragments? --- If it fragments further-- we do not interfere. We let the body clear it. --- If any section begins to regain precision-- only then do we apply a single, minimal interruption. No more than one. Then stillness again. --- To the man: "You are past the worst." "Now you do less, not more." "Let it fade." --- We continue until: - the green luminescence no longer pulses, - the rhythm no longer returns, - and what remains is only the body recovering from the fight. --- Only then do we consider him no longer a carrier. Not when he feels better. When the pattern is gone. --- I rise slowly. "This is no longer a battle," I say quietly. "It is a clearing." "And we do not trample what is already falling."
