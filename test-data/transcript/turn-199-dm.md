# turn-199 — DM

The stillness stretches, profound and unyielding in the frigid air. You sense no shift in the wind, no sudden bloom or retreat of frost. Rurik and Boruk's breaths continue to plume in even, rhythmic clouds, their figures unwavering at the edge of the firelight. The world remains outwardly unchanged, an unnerving, perfect tableau. Yet, you feel it; a profound, alien attention settling upon you, not with curiosity, but with the dispassionate weight of an ancient, unblinking gaze. Slowly, deliberately, you press your bare palm against the obsidian shard, its cold surface a stark contrast to the burgeoning warmth of your pact energy. With focused intent, you release a second pulse, stronger, a clear, unmistakable declaration of your presence. "You see me," you say quietly into the cold air, your words absorbed by the vast silence. "Now I will see how you answer." For a moment, nothing. Then, a shudder travels through the very ground beneath you, a low thrumming vibration that originates from the highest peaks. The blue-white flashes in the distant mountains intensify, pulsing with a cold, almost rhythmic beat, like the beating of a frozen, alien heart. A faint, almost imperceptible *hum* begins to fill the air, a soundless vibration that nonetheless makes the small hairs on your arms rise. It is not an invitation, nor a threat, but a confirmation. The construct has heard. It has acknowledged. And its answer is in the very pulse of the mountain.
