# turn-037 — DM

Your eyes, accustomed to discerning details in the deepest gloom, sweep across the camp with newfound clarity. You notice the broad shoulders of the hunters, their movements heavy and practiced as they mend snares and clean pelts. Nearer the fire, the women are engaged in smaller, more delicate tasks - scraping hides, weaving simple baskets from dried reeds, and meticulously sorting through bundles of dried herbs and roots, some of which resemble the dark material you'd been offered. You observe the watchman on the edge of the camp, his posture rigid, scanning the surrounding forest for any sign of movement. The elder, as if sensing your gaze, turns his head slowly. His eyes meet yours, and a faint, almost imperceptible nod acknowledges your keen observation. He gestures towards the very edge of the camp, where the firelight barely penetrates, then points to a small, intricate carving tool lying near the woman sorting herbs. It's a subtle invitation, suggesting a task requiring a lighter touch, perhaps more suited to your apparent disposition than the brute strength of splitting wood.
