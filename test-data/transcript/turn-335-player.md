# turn-335 — PLAYER

You do not answer her in front of the others. You rise slowly, adjusting Rune in your arms, then pass her gently to Lena. Your gaze returns to Maelis, steady, not appraising--recognizing. "Walk with me." You do not wait for agreement. You step beyond the longhouse, into the crisp edge of winter light, where the sounds of the Quiet Weave soften into distance. Snow compresses underfoot. No audience. No expectation. For a time, you say nothing. Then, quietly: "You saw clearly." A pause. "And you spoke without trying to please me. That matters." You glance at her--not as a leader to a recruit, but as one woman measuring another who has carried weight alone. "In this place, there are already two who lead in ways the world does not expect." You gesture lightly back toward the longhouse. "Lyrawyn. She sees what most cannot. She will become something like a sage, though she does not yet know the word." Then, after a breath: "And me. I build. Not just structures, not just plans. I build what holds people together when everything else fails." You let that settle, then continue--more quietly, more honestly. "But the hunt... and war... have remained with the men." You look out across the boundary fields, where the patterns lie beneath the snow. "Not because women cannot. Because no one chose to change it." You turn back to her fully now. "I am choosing to change it." No force in the statement. Only certainty. "We have learned something here. Every time we allowed difference--true difference--we became stronger. Not weaker. Not divided. Stronger." Your gaze sharpens slightly. "Not everyone must do everything. But no one should be forbidden from what they could become." A small step closer--not closing distance, but aligning with her. "You asked how women like you can become part of this." A pause. "Not by fitting into what exists." "By shaping what does not yet exist." You let the wind pass between you before continuing. "Gorok came as you did. He tested. He resisted. He saw what held." You glance back toward where he stands in the distance. "He brought us strength we did not have. Not better. Not worse. Different. Necessary." Your voice lowers slightly. "You are the same kind of arrival." Not praise. Recognition. "We do not have what you are." "And if we try to turn you into what we already are... we lose it." You let silence sit again, giving her space inside the thought. Then, more gently: "If women learn the hunt... the fight... the same way children here learn to build, to see, to think..." "Then what we create will not depend on one kind of strength surviving." Your eyes hold hers now--direct, but not pressing. "It will endure because it has many." A final pause. "You do not need to decide who you are here." "You only need to decide if you are willing to shape something that does not yet have a name." You step back slightly, releasing the moment. "No one will take your people from you." "But if you choose this... what you build here will reach them." You turn just enough to begin walking back. "Walk with me again when you have your answer." And you leave her with it--not a role, not a demand-- --but a space only she can choose to fill.
