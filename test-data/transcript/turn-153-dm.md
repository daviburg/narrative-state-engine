# turn-153 — DM

Wrapped within the longhouse, the wild greens you gathered become the focus of your next culinary experiment. You meticulously chop the arctic dock, fireweed shoots, and wild mustard, layering them with salt in sturdy ceramic crocks you've discovered among the tribe's belongings. The process is one of patient observation and subtle influence, much like your mead-making. You press the mixture, allowing the natural fermentation to begin, constantly adjusting and tasting. Weeks pass in this careful art, and slowly, a pungent, yet invigorating aroma begins to permeate the air - a clear sign of success. The sour, tangy flavor of your first sauerkraut is a triumph, a vital new source of nutrients for the tribe. Once you are confident in your method, you seek out a new apprentice, someone eager to learn the secrets of this new preservation technique. Perhaps it is one of the women who helped you forage, her curiosity piqued by your strange concoctions. As for your loins, the weeks since Lyrawyn's birth have brought a gradual return of strength and vitality. The demanding rhythms of motherhood have naturally eased, and the tenderness of earlier days has faded. Your body, resilient and eager, signals its readiness for renewed intimacy. The sight of Kael, strong and devoted, stirs a familiar longing, and the dream of more children, of a thriving lineage to secure the future of the tribe, burns bright within you, a powerful, guiding flame.
