# turn-219 — DM

Winter holds the land in an unyielding grip, but within the longhouse, a different kind of force begins to take shape. You clear a small space near the entrance where the fragment rests. Upon a flat stone, you draw a simple, intentional symbol in charcoal, a deliberate contrast to the mountain's geometric perfection. The wrapped fragment is placed at its center. Anya approaches, her small face serious, her eyes fixed on you. "You will not reach for the mountain," you tell her quietly. "You will repeat what you can already do." You indicate the charcoal symbol. "Trace this in frost. No more. No less." Precision, not ambition, is the focus. You remain close, a silent, watchful presence. The Shaman stands by, his ancient gaze fixed on Anya, while Kael, a formidable silhouette, stands at the edge of the small circle, his attention unwavering. This is not a spectacle; it is a test. Anya closes her eyes, her brow furrowed in concentration. Her breath mists in the cold air of the longhouse, and her small hand extends towards the stone. A thin, shimmering line of frost begins to form, tracing the charcoal symbol with an almost unnatural speed and clarity. It is perfect, sharper, more defined than any frost she has created before. It doesn't just trace the charcoal; it seems to *etch* itself into the very stone, leaving a faint, almost indelible impression. As the frost settles, Fenouille, you feel it: a subtle, chilling amplification radiating from the fragment. It doesn't distort Anya's control, but rather, it supercharges it, lending her nascent power a cold, almost sterile precision that echoes the mountain's own influence. The air around the fragment itself feels colder, the water in the nearby vessels showing minute, almost crystalline patterns of freezing where there should be none. The Shaman lets out a low, almost inaudible hum, his eyes widening. Kael remains impassive, but his posture subtly shifts, acknowledging the unseen power at play. "We do not ask the mountain to answer," you say softly, your gaze sweeping from the unnaturally sharp frost to the subtly vibrating fragment, then to Anya, whose eyes now hold a new, awe-struck understanding. "We see what changes when we act beneath its gaze." The mountain may not speak, but through Anya, it has revealed its capacity to influence, to shape, and to empower in its own chilling, precise way.
