# turn-330 — DM

"Kael," you say, your voice steady, grounded again in structure, "this is not a wandering. This is a defined path." You call forward Tala, her quiet competence evident as she steps towards you, her expression one of focused readiness. "She goes with you." You look between them, ensuring your intent is clear. "You are not going to search blindly. You are going to speak, observe, and choose. Kael, you judge strength. Not only in body, but in resolve. Tala, you judge alignment. Whether they can stand within what we are without breaking it." Neither alone is sufficient; together, they will decide. You set the boundaries. "You do not promise anything on my behalf. You do not bind anyone before they stand here. You invite. Nothing more." Then you define the timeline with unyielding precision. "You return before the birth. No exception. If the snow deepens, you turn back. If either of you feels your life is at risk, you turn back. This mission strengthens nothing if it costs what we already have." You narrow the objective further, ensuring no room for misinterpretation. "You are not looking for many. You are looking for the right ones. One is enough. More is risk." Stepping closer, you emphasize the subtle nuances of their search. "And you observe more than warriors. You look for women who already stand apart. Those who lead without title. Those who are not easily bent. Those who would not be diminished by standing among us--but would grow, and make us grow." The final, crucial constraint follows. "You do not bring conflict back with you. If a tribe resists, you leave them unchanged. This is not expansion. This is selection." You look at them both, their faces etched with the gravity and profound implications of the task. Kael, his gaze now resolute, nods slowly, his understanding complete. Tala, though quiet, mirrors his determination, her eyes reflecting a keen discernment. The path is now defined, awaiting their first steps at dawn.
