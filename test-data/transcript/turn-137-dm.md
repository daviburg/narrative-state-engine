# turn-137 — DM

With the longhouse complete and a new sense of permanence settling over the tribe, your thoughts turn beyond immediate survival to sustained prosperity. You begin to articulate your vision, carefully explaining through gestures, the few words you've learned, and crude drawings in the packed snow, the concept of cultivation. You point to the earth around the longhouse, then mimic planting seeds, conveying the idea of edible plants and herbs growing close at hand. The women, who already tend to wild foraging, grasp the concept of bringing these valuable resources closer, and a small, sheltered patch of ground is tentatively prepared, awaiting the spring thaw. Your gaze then extends to the nearby forest. With Kael and a few older hunters, you journey to select areas rich with nut and berry trees. You demonstrate, not with arcane power but with observation, how clearing away competing brush will allow these vital food sources to flourish. The men, ever practical, understand the wisdom in nurturing future harvests. You then present the hunters with ideas for diversifying their hunting strategies. Bigger game, new techniques - concepts that spark intense discussions among the seasoned warriors. You suggest to the children, already keen observers of the wild, the placement of small traps near watering holes, turning play into practical skill. The frozen lake becomes a canvas for another idea: drilling holes, patiently waiting for the unseen bounty beneath the ice. Your vision begins to transform the tribe's perspective, planting seeds of change that promise a more abundant future, all while the life within you grows, a constant, comforting reminder of why you build this new world.
