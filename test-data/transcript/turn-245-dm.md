"Did the heat lessen, even for a breath? Did the pain move? Did anything inside you stop--or shift?" He strains to answer, his voice a dry rasp. "The fire... it flickered..." he whispers, "just for a moment. And the buzzing... in my head... it was quieter." His lived sense of the pattern confirms your own observations. The pattern has not fully recovered. You look to Kael, your gaze firm. "Hold the boundary." Then to the healer, "Mark every change." Quietly, to yourself, you acknowledge: "It can stumble. You will win by making it lose its shape.
