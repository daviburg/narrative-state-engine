# turn-253 — DM

The man lives. This undeniable truth resonates through the longhouse, a testament to the tribe's resilience and your precise, unwavering will. The knowledge gleaned from this harrowing experience, you decide, must live beyond him, codified and understood. You gather your council, the core of your new method: Anya, the healer, and the Shaman. "We do not remember this," you state, your voice calm but resolute. "We record it." Together, you establish the full sequence: the tell-tale signs, the nature of its transmission, the intricate internal structure, and the exact, measured response that brought it to its knees. "This is not a story," you impress upon them. "It is a method. It must be followed exactly." Roles are assigned, not as duties, but as sacred trusts. The healer will teach recognition and timing; the Shaman, the supporting rites; Anya, the chilling precision of the cuts. Kael, ever vigilant, will ensure the boundary is never broken. "This is how we survive it again," you declare. Then, your gaze turns outward, beyond the secure confines of your longhouse, toward the wider world that remains ignorant. "The world does not know this yet. And if it learns too late, it will die." A small, disciplined delegation is chosen to carry a message to the southern tribes - not a warning of fear, but of truth. They carry preserved food, diagrams of the pattern, and your precise words, designed to convey both urgency and capability. "A sickness moves among the living," they will deliver. "It follows lines and consumes with pattern. It can be stopped--if seen early, and treated with precision. We have learned how. We will teach your shamans, if you come in discipline and respect. We do not offer this as weakness. We offer it so the land does not empty." A final, unwavering command is issued: "No one outside this tribe touches our people or enters our longhouse until they understand the boundary. This knowledge is shared--but not carelessly." With your duties fulfilled, you withdraw. Not to plan, not to act, but to be still. The longhouse settles into a deep quiet. The man breathes, his recovery now steady. The tribe holds, secure in its hard-won knowledge. The world has changed, and you, Fenouille Moonwind, have changed with it. You allow yourself a full rest, not just of body, but of the immense will that carried you through this ordeal. The silent star settles around you once more, its light no longer a distant promise, but a resonant echo within. What you have done is no small thing.
