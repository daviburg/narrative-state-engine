# turn-083 — DM

As you watch the shaman move, you instinctively reach out with your senses, not with your eyes, but with the subtle tendrils of your own pact-granted power. There is no flamboyant burst of arcane energy, no overt spellcasting, yet you feel a distinct resonance. It's a deep, earthy magic, woven tightly into the fabric of the land itself - the chill wind, the ancient snow, the stark, towering pines. It feels primal, rooted, quite unlike the cosmic hum of your own celestial pact, but undoubtedly powerful. It's a magic born of reverence and survival, a whisper from the spirits of the wild rather than a shout from the stars. There's a fundamental understanding there, a shared language of power, even if the dialects are vastly different. The shaman glances in your direction, his obsidian-topped staff tapping lightly on the packed snow, a faint, knowing smile playing on his lips. He does not approach, but his gaze seems to invite a connection, an unspoken acknowledgment of your own unique presence in his world.
