# turn-329 — PLAYER

I do not let the idea expand unchecked. I shape it. "Kael," I say, my voice steady, grounded again in structure, "this is not a wandering. This is a defined path." I call another forward. Not the strongest. Not the quietest. The one who speaks well, who listens, who others trust quickly. Tala steps forward. "She goes with you." I look between them. "You are not going to search blindly." "You are going to speak, observe, and choose." I define their roles clearly. "Kael, you judge strength. Not only in body, but in resolve." "Tala, you judge alignment. Whether they can stand within what we are without breaking it." Neither alone is sufficient. Together, they decide. I set the boundary. "You do not promise anything on my behalf." "You do not bind anyone before they stand here." "You invite. Nothing more." Then I define time. "You return before the birth." "No exception." "If the snow deepens, you turn back." "If either of you feels your life is at risk, you turn back." "This mission strengthens nothing if it costs what we already have." I let that settle. Then I narrow the objective. "You are not looking for many." "You are looking for the right ones." "One is enough." "More is risk." I step closer. "And you observe more than warriors." "You look for women who already stand apart." "Those who lead without title." "Those who are not easily bent." "Those who would not be diminished by standing among us--but would grow, and make us grow." I pause, then add the final constraint. "You do not bring conflict back with you." "If a tribe resists, you leave them unchanged." I step back. "This is not expansion." "This is selection." I look at them both. "You leave at first light." "And you return with truth--not hope." I do not say more. The path is now defined.
