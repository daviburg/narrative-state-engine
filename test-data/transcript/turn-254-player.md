# turn-254 — PLAYER

We do not remain still after this. What we have built must now meet the world. --- Time passes again--not in waiting, but in motion across many fronts. We do not focus on one path. We let all paths move, and we observe what answers first. --- The man continues his recovery under the healer's watch. We observe: - whether any trace of the pattern returns, - whether his strength rebuilds steadily, - and whether he retains memory of where he came from and what he saw. If he is to be the first survivor, he is also our first witness. --- The delegation to the southern tribes departs. We do not follow them. We wait to see: - whether they are received with trust, suspicion, or urgency, - whether their shamans respond to our method, - and whether the sickness has already reached them. Their reaction will tell us more than any report. --- The rivers begin to break. I walk their edges. Not far--but enough to see change. I look for: - signs of the trapper's path, - any unusual stones in the thawing water, - and whether the land itself carries signs of sickness or absence. If knowledge travels by river, it will surface here. --- At the fragment, observation continues. Now that the season has shifted, I watch for change: - does its rhythm alter with the thaw, - does it react to the absence of the pattern we broke, - and does Anya's control behave differently now that she has used it against something living? I do not intervene. I observe what has changed. --- Anya continues her training--but now with purpose. Not only precision. Application. I have her repeat controlled, minimal disruptions on inert materials, on frost, on patterns we create-- to ensure what we used once can be used again without risk. --- Within the tribe, I watch something quieter. Lyrawyn learns patterns. Faelan grows. And within me, life continues to take shape. Strength is not only what we build outward. It is what continues forward. --- We allow this span of time to unfold. Not indefinitely. Only long enough for each of these paths to answer in its own way. --- Then I gather what has changed. Not what we hoped. What has actually moved. And I decide where we act next.
