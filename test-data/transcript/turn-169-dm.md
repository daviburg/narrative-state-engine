# turn-169 — DM

Your thoughts, ever practical, shift from external threats to internal vulnerabilities. Fire, the very heart of the longhouse's warmth and sustenance, also presents its greatest danger. You envision a large, sturdy water barrel, always kept full, positioned strategically just outside the longhouse's main entrance but far enough to remain safe should the building itself ignite. This would require regular refilling, especially in the freezing temperatures, but the foresight could save everything. You discuss this with Kael, explaining the grim necessity, and he nods, his brow furrowed in thoughtful agreement, already calculating the logistics. Simultaneously, your mind races to the unthinkable: a direct assault. In such a dire event, the non-combatants, especially the children, would need a safe haven. You begin to search for a natural recess, perhaps a small, deep cave hidden by snowdrifts, or even a cleverly reinforced underground dugout, close enough for quick access but well-concealed from prying eyes. It must be defensible, or at least impossible to easily find. You sketch out crude plans in the snow for a camouflaged entrance, a place where the tribe's most vulnerable could take refuge while the warriors, including yourself, stood their ground. This, too, you convey to Kael and, through him, to the Elder, highlighting the absolute necessity of such a sanctuary.
