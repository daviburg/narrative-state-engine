# turn-278 — DM

"If two divide it," you say quietly, your gaze fixed on the landscape, "then three must define it." You keep both existing disruptions precisely as they are, two steady, unwavering points of imperfection. You select a third point, deliberately chosen not to align, but to form a wide triangle that opens towards your position and closes toward the river bend. "The same as before," you instruct Anya, "No deeper. No wider. Only once." She executes the command with her characteristic, almost ethereal precision, a third faint, icy disruption blooming on the thawing ground. The Shaman's Echo-Song continues its low, constant hum, a persistent resonance against the alien structure. You step back, your focus now piercing the space *inside* the defined triangle. The effect is profound. Within the triangle's bounds, the hexagonal melt patterns, once subtly pervasive, now struggle to assert themselves, appearing fragmented and fleeting. The crystalline growths, which had shimmered with sterile beauty, now seem to retract visibly, receding from the edges of the triangular zone, leaving behind patches of relatively untouched, thawing earth. You even notice, with a thrill of quiet triumph, faint deer tracks venturing slightly further into this 'cleared' space than they would dare elsewhere, tentative explorers of a new, softer boundary. The system does not attempt to "route around" the triangle. Instead, it seems to bleed correction into it, attempting to maintain some semblance of its patterned presence within the defined region. However, this diversion of energy clearly thins its influence elsewhere. Outside the triangle, the shimmer of hexagonal melts and the presence of crystalline growths, while still present, seem fractionally weaker, their edges less defined. The system is stretching itself, struggling to maintain its pervasive order. Most importantly, the river stones react with a palpable instability. The blue pulse now stutters and flickers with alarming frequency, sometimes dimming significantly before recovering. The rhythmic desynchronization observed with two disruptions is now a jarring discord, as if the central source is struggling to maintain a consistent output. There is a noticeable drop in overall intensity, a loss of its former vibrant certainty. You hold for a full interval, allowing the profound changes to settle into the landscape. "If this holds," you say quietly, a deep satisfaction in your voice, "then we do not fight the system. We draw where it cannot be." The system has revealed a critical weakness: it cannot tolerate a defined region of sustained imperfection without bleeding energy and losing coherence. You have not broken it, but you have found its limits.
