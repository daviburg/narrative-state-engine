Your daily rounds take you through the vibrant, ever-busier longhouse. Tala, your first mead apprentice, now works with practiced ease, her movements fluid as she tends the large fermentation crocks. The rich, sweet aroma of honeyed mead confirms her mastery; she requires little, if any, oversight. Elara, focused on the sauerkraut, still shows a methodical diligence, carefully salting and pressing the gathered greens. She has gained a confident rhythm, though a quick word of encouragement or a subtle adjustment from you still occasionally helps refine her touch. Lena, your co-teacher, has embraced her role with quiet enthusiasm, her calm presence proving invaluable in managing the growing number of eager young minds. She now takes the lead on some lessons, her gentle voice guiding the children through the geometric shapes in the snow. Outside, the hunters, led by Kael, have subtly shifted their methods. While still relying on their ancestral knowledge, there's a more communal approach to planning, a greater emphasis on efficiency and sustainability, driven by the needs of a growing, more settled population. You hear talk of expanding traps and coordinating larger drives. As for the cultivated plants, near the longhouse, where the earth has been carefully disturbed and enriched, tiny, fragile shoots are beginning to poke through the still-cold soil - a testament to patient effort and a fragile hope for future harvests, though they are still far from robust.
