# turn-258 — PLAYER

Before we leave, I do not let any part of this remain assumed. We stop and take account. Not of intention. Of result. --- I stand before the assembled tribe, the southern shamans, and those chosen for the journey. "Before first light becomes departure," I say, "we name what is real." --- First: the teaching. I watch the southern shamans complete the method once under our eyes. Not in theory. In practice. I want to see: - whether they keep the boundary, - whether they follow the timing, - whether they understand precision instead of force, - and whether the patient improves in their hands as he did in ours. If they fail, they do not leave with the method. If they succeed, then trust begins. --- Second: the witness. I sit with the survivor and study what he has drawn. I do not ask for stories. I ask for sequence. - where the sickness first appeared, - how it spread, - where the dead were left, - where the stones were found, - and what route he took when he fled. If there is a true beginning, I want it marked clearly enough that we can walk toward it instead of wandering into it. --- Third: the fragment. Before departure, I observe it one final time beside the Shaman. Not to test it. To compare it to what it was before. - Has its rhythm changed further? - Does it react to the shamans, the cured man, or the teaching? - Does it pull toward the east, the thaw, or the stones? I want to know what has shifted before I carry my own body closer to its source. --- Fourth: the equipment. I lay out what has actually been made and traded. I examine each piece in hand. - the head covering, - the off-hand tool, - the ranged option, - any charm, ward, or carried protection from the southern shamans, - and the healer's selected travel supplies. If something is weak, awkward, loud, or untrusted, I leave it behind. I depart with what works, not what was merely offered. --- Fifth: the expedition. I look at each who will walk east with me. Not just whether they are willing. Whether they are ready. Each carries only what serves the journey. Each knows the boundary, the sickness, and the rule: we go to learn, not to prove courage. --- Only when all five are answered do I give the final word. If the teaching held, if the witness has given us a true path, if the fragment has been read, if the equipment is real, and if the company is ready-- then we leave at first light. Otherwise, we delay only long enough to correct what is unfinished. Now show me what stands ready, what failed, what was gained, and what must still be decided before we depart.
