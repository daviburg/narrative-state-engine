# turn-151 — DM

Bundled against the biting cold, you venture out, your eyes keenly scanning the snow-dusted terrain. Your elven senses, combined with a newfound intimacy with this land, guide your search. You move with purpose, occasionally nudging aside a drift with your digging stick. Beneath a rocky outcrop, sheltered from the harshest winds, you find it: a cluster of hardy arctic dock, its broad, resilient leaves a dark green against the snow. Further on, near a frozen stream bed, the distinctive reddish stems of fireweed shoots push through a thin layer of ice, hinting at the vitality beneath. And finally, in a more exposed, wind-swept area, a tenacious patch of wild mustard, its small, serrated leaves clinging stubbornly to life. You carefully harvest enough of each, mindful not to deplete the patches entirely, knowing they represent a valuable resource. Your basket grows heavy, filled with the promise of future fermentations. The successful forage invigorates you, a small victory against the harshness of the arctic, and a tangible step towards your next project.
