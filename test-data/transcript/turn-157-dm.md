# turn-157 — DM

With a new life stirring within you, your days take on a rhythm of quiet anticipation and purposeful action. Your first student is, of course, Lyrawyn, her bright eyes following your movements with an intense curiosity. You begin by composing simple songs, weaving numbers into repetitive, catchy melodies. The tribal children, drawn by the unusual sounds and your gentle presence, slowly gather, their initial shyness giving way to fascination. You teach them counting, not as abstract symbols, but as the steady beat of a drum, the number of fingers held up, the rhythm of footsteps in the snow. As the children grow more comfortable, you introduce a dance - a seemingly silly series of stomps, claps, and turns. What they don't realize is that beneath the playful movements lies a subtle, intricate pattern of counting steps, of grouping movements, of understanding sequences. Lyrawyn, even in her young age, quickly picks up the cadence, her small body swaying in time. Soon, a small group of young ones eagerly awaits your daily lessons, their laughter echoing in the longhouse, their minds subtly opening to the logic of numbers, guided by your innovative blend of joy and learning. The elder watches from afar, a flicker of something unreadable in his flinty eyes.
