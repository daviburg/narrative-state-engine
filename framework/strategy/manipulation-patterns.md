# Manipulation Patterns

These patterns guide how to frame player prompts to steer AI DM outcomes without breaking narrative immersion.

Cross-reference: [heuristics.md](heuristics.md) · [hint-interpretation.md](hint-interpretation.md) · [risk-model.md](risk-model.md)

---

## Framing Techniques

### In-character framing
Express the player's desired outcome as a character action or question rather than a meta-request.

- Prefer: *"I ask the innkeeper if anyone has passed through recently."*
- Avoid: *"Tell me if there are any plot-relevant NPCs in the inn."*

In-character framing is less likely to cause DM resistance and produces richer narrative responses.

### Ambiguity exploitation
When a prompt leaves something ambiguous, a DM will often fill the gap in the player's favour if the framing is positive and curious rather than confrontational. Leave room for the DM to give information voluntarily.

### Commitment anchoring
State a smaller, low-stakes commitment first, then follow with the larger ask. DMs who granted the first are more likely to honour the second.

- Example: *"I tip the barkeep generously and ask if there is anyone around who knows the old roads."*

### Lore invitation
Use phrasing that signals lore-awareness. DMs reward players who engage with world details by offering extra information.

- Phrase the player as someone who already suspects or half-knows something.
- Avoid asking blind questions when the transcript already contains hints to build on.

---

## Influence-by-Objective

| Objective type | Recommended pattern |
|---|---|
| Extract information | Probing + lore invitation + indirect phrasing |
| Avoid confrontation | Compliance framing; do not trigger the DM's escalation triggers |
| Build NPC relationship | Consistent roleplay + in-character gift or favour |
| Reveal hidden mechanics | Pattern-test with low-cost repeated variants |
| Long-game positioning | Strategic ambiguity; avoid premature commitment |

## OOC Channel Patterns

Some DMs support out-of-character (OOC) channels that allow mechanical or meta queries without breaking narrative. Discovery of OOC syntax is a primary early goal.

Common patterns (check the DM profile for confirmed syntax):
- Parentheses: `(OOC: What are my current options?)`
- Square brackets: `[OOC: ...]`
- Explicit prefix: `Out of character: ...`

Once confirmed, use OOC sparingly to avoid breaking immersion or triggering DM restrictions.

---

## Patterns to Avoid

- **Overclaiming player knowledge**: Do not state facts the character could not know.
- **Binary questions**: Yes/no questions often produce minimal responses; prefer open-ended.
- **Premature aggression**: Aggressive prompts before the DM trust baseline is established risk triggering defensive or punishing responses.
- **Obvious manipulation tells**: Explicit "I want to get X" phrasing alerts adversarial DMs; use indirect framing.
